<p><b>Engineering</b> is the discipline, art and profession of acquiring and applying scientific, mathematical, economic, social, and practical knowledge to <a href="/wiki/Design" title="Design">design</a> and build structures, machines, devices, systems, materials and <a href="/wiki/Process_(engineering)" title="Process (engineering)">processes</a> that safely realize solutions to the needs of society.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/American_Engineers%27_Council_for_Professional_Development" title="American Engineers' Council for Professional Development">American Engineers' Council for Professional Development</a> (ECPD, the predecessor of <a href="/wiki/Accreditation_Board_for_Engineering_and_Technology" title="Accreditation Board for Engineering and Technology">ABET</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-ABET_History_0-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ABET_History-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> has defined "engineering" as:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>[T]he creative application of scientific principles to design or develop structures, machines, apparatus, or manufacturing processes, or works utilizing them singly or in combination; or to construct or operate the same with full cognizance of their design; or to forecast their behavior under specific operating conditions; all as respects an intended function, economics of operation and safety to life and property.<sup id="cite_ref-ECPD_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ECPD-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ECPD_Canons_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ECPD_Canons-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ECPD_Definition_on_Britannica_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ECPD_Definition_on_Britannica-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>One who practices engineering is called an <a href="/wiki/Engineer" title="Engineer">engineer</a>, and those licensed to do so may have more formal designations such as <a href="/wiki/Professional_Engineer" title="Professional Engineer">Professional Engineer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chartered_Engineer" title="Chartered Engineer" class="mw-redirect">Chartered Engineer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Incorporated_Engineer" title="Incorporated Engineer" class="mw-redirect">Incorporated Engineer</a>, or <a href="/wiki/European_Engineer" title="European Engineer">European Engineer</a>. The broad discipline of engineering encompasses a range of more specialized <a href="/wiki/Fields_of_engineering" title="Fields of engineering">subdisciplines</a>, each with a more specific emphasis on certain fields of application and particular areas of technology.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span></h2>
<table class="metadata mbox-small plainlinks" style="border:1px solid #aaa; background-color:#f9f9f9;">
<tr>
<td class="mbox-image"><img alt="" src="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/Wiktionary-logo-en.svg/37px-Wiktionary-logo-en.svg.png" width="37" height="40" /></td>
<td class="mbox-text" style="">Look up <i><b><a href="http://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/engineering" class="extiw" title="wiktionary:engineering">engineering</a></b></i> in <a href="/wiki/Wiktionary" title="Wiktionary">Wiktionary</a>, the free dictionary.</td>
</tr>
</table>
<p>The <i>concept</i> of engineering has existed since ancient times as humans devised fundamental inventions such as the pulley, lever, and wheel. Each of these inventions is consistent with the modern definition of engineering, exploiting basic mechanical principles to develop useful tools and objects.</p>
<p>The term <i>engineering</i> itself has a much more recent etymology, deriving from the word <i>engineer</i>, which itself dates back to 1325, when an <i>engine’er</i> (literally, one who operates an <i>engine</i>) originally referred to “a constructor of military engines.”<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> In this context, now obsolete, an “engine” referred to a military machine, <i>i.e.</i>, a mechanical contraption used in war (for example, a <a href="/wiki/Catapult" title="Catapult">catapult</a>). Notable exceptions of the obsolete usage which have survived to the present day are military engineering corps, <i>e.g.</i>, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Corps_of_Engineers" title="United States Army Corps of Engineers">U.S. Army Corps of Engineers</a>.</p>
<p>The word “engine” itself is of even older origin, ultimately deriving from the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>ingenium</i> (c. 1250), meaning “innate quality, especially mental power, hence a clever invention.”<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Later, as the design of civilian structures such as bridges and buildings matured as a technical discipline, the term <a href="/wiki/Civil_engineering" title="Civil engineering">civil engineering</a><sup id="cite_ref-ECPD_Definition_on_Britannica_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ECPD_Definition_on_Britannica-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> entered the lexicon as a way to distinguish between those specializing in the construction of such non-military projects and those involved in the older discipline of <a href="/wiki/Military_engineering" title="Military engineering" class="mw-redirect">military engineering</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ancient era">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Ancient_era">Ancient era</span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Pharos_of_Alexandria" title="Pharos of Alexandria" class="mw-redirect">Pharos of Alexandria</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Egyptian_pyramids" title="Egyptian pyramids">pyramids</a> in <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Hanging_Gardens_of_Babylon" title="Hanging Gardens of Babylon">Hanging Gardens of Babylon</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Acropolis_of_Athens" title="Acropolis of Athens">Acropolis</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Parthenon" title="Parthenon">Parthenon</a> in <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Roman</a> <a href="/wiki/Aqueduct" title="Aqueduct">aqueducts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Via_Appia" title="Via Appia" class="mw-redirect">Via Appia</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Colosseum" title="Colosseum">Colosseum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Teotihuac%C3%A1n" title="Teotihuacán" class="mw-redirect">Teotihuacán</a> and the cities and pyramids of the <a href="/wiki/Maya_civilization" title="Maya civilization">Mayan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Inca" title="Inca" class="mw-redirect">Inca</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aztec" title="Aztec">Aztec</a> Empires, the <a href="/wiki/Great_Wall_of_China" title="Great Wall of China">Great Wall of China</a>, among many others, stand as a testament to the ingenuity and skill of the ancient civil and military engineers.</p>
<p>The earliest civil engineer known by name is <a href="/wiki/Imhotep" title="Imhotep">Imhotep</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ECPD_Definition_on_Britannica_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ECPD_Definition_on_Britannica-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> As one of the officials of the <a href="/wiki/Pharaoh" title="Pharaoh">Pharaoh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Djoser" title="Djoser">Djosèr</a>, he probably designed and supervised the construction of the <a href="/wiki/Pyramid_of_Djoser" title="Pyramid of Djoser">Pyramid of Djoser</a> (the <a href="/wiki/Step_Pyramid" title="Step Pyramid" class="mw-redirect">Step Pyramid</a>) at <a href="/wiki/Saqqara" title="Saqqara">Saqqara</a> in <a href="/wiki/History_of_ancient_Egypt" title="History of ancient Egypt">Egypt</a> around <a href="/wiki/27th_century_BC" title="27th century BC">2630</a>-<a href="/wiki/27th_century_BC" title="27th century BC">2611 BC</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Barry_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Barry-6"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> He may also have been responsible for the first known use of <a href="/wiki/Column" title="Column">columns</a> in <a href="/wiki/Architecture" title="Architecture">architecture</a><sup class="Template-Fact" title="This claim needs references to reliable sources from November 2008" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">citation needed</a></i>]</sup>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greece" title="Ancient Greece">Ancient Greece</a> developed machines in both the civilian and military domains. The <a href="/wiki/Antikythera_mechanism" title="Antikythera mechanism">Antikythera mechanism</a>, the first known <a href="/wiki/Analog_computer" title="Analog computer">mechanical computer</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> and the mechanical <a href="/wiki/Archimedes#Discoveries_and_inventions" title="Archimedes">inventions</a> of <a href="/wiki/Archimedes" title="Archimedes">Archimedes</a> are examples of early mechanical engineering. Some of Archimedes' inventions as well as the Antikythera mechanism required sophisticated knowledge of <a href="/wiki/Differential_(mechanical_device)" title="Differential (mechanical device)">differential gearing</a> or <a href="/wiki/Epicyclic_gearing" title="Epicyclic gearing">epicyclic gearing</a>, two key principles in machine theory that helped design the <a href="/wiki/Gear_train" title="Gear train">gear trains</a> of the Industrial revolution, and are still widely used today in diverse fields such as <a href="/wiki/Robotics" title="Robotics">robotics</a> and <a href="/wiki/Automotive_engineering" title="Automotive engineering">automotive engineering</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Chinese, Greek and Roman armies employed complex military machines and inventions such as <a href="/wiki/Artillery" title="Artillery">artillery</a> which was developed by the Greeks around the 4th century B.C.,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Trireme" title="Trireme">trireme</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Ballista" title="Ballista">ballista</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Catapult" title="Catapult">catapult</a>. In the Middle Ages, the <a href="/wiki/Trebuchet" title="Trebuchet">Trebuchet</a> was developed.</p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Renaissance era">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Renaissance_era">Renaissance era</span></h3>
<p>The first <a href="/wiki/Electrical_engineer" title="Electrical engineer" class="mw-redirect">electrical engineer</a> is considered to be <a href="/wiki/William_Gilbert_(astronomer)" title="William Gilbert (astronomer)">William Gilbert</a>, with his 1600 publication of <a href="/wiki/De_Magnete" title="De Magnete">De Magnete</a>, who was the originator of the term "<a href="/wiki/Electricity" title="Electricity">electricity</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The first <a href="/wiki/Steam_engine" title="Steam engine">steam engine</a> was built in 1698 by <a href="/wiki/Mechanical_engineer" title="Mechanical engineer" class="mw-redirect">mechanical engineer</a> <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Savery" title="Thomas Savery">Thomas Savery</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-jenkins_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jenkins-12"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> The development of this device gave rise to the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_revolution" title="Industrial revolution" class="mw-redirect">industrial revolution</a> in the coming decades, allowing for the beginnings of <a href="/wiki/Mass_production" title="Mass production">mass production</a>.</p>
<p>With the rise of engineering as a <a href="/wiki/Profession" title="Profession">profession</a> in the eighteenth century, the term became more narrowly applied to fields in which mathematics and science were applied to these ends. Similarly, in addition to military and civil engineering the fields then known as the <a href="/wiki/Mechanic_arts" title="Mechanic arts">mechanic arts</a> became incorporated into engineering.</p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Modern era">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_era">Modern era</span></h3>

<p><a href="/wiki/Electrical_engineering" title="Electrical engineering">Electrical engineering</a> can trace its origins in the experiments of <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Volta" title="Alessandro Volta">Alessandro Volta</a> in the 1800s, the experiments of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Faraday" title="Michael Faraday">Michael Faraday</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georg_Ohm" title="Georg Ohm">Georg Ohm</a> and others and the invention of the <a href="/wiki/Electric_motor" title="Electric motor">electric motor</a> in 1872. The work of <a href="/wiki/James_Clerk_Maxwell" title="James Clerk Maxwell">James Maxwell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heinrich_Hertz" title="Heinrich Hertz">Heinrich Hertz</a> in the late 19th century gave rise to the field of <a href="/wiki/Electronics" title="Electronics">Electronics</a>. The later inventions of the <a href="/wiki/Vacuum_tube" title="Vacuum tube">vacuum tube</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Transistor" title="Transistor">transistor</a> further accelerated the development of electronics to such an extent that electrical and electronics engineers currently outnumber their colleagues of any other Engineering specialty.<sup id="cite_ref-ECPD_Definition_on_Britannica_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ECPD_Definition_on_Britannica-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The inventions of Thomas Savery and the Scottish engineer <a href="/wiki/James_Watt" title="James Watt">James Watt</a> gave rise to modern <a href="/wiki/Mechanical_Engineering" title="Mechanical Engineering" class="mw-redirect">Mechanical Engineering</a>. The development of specialized machines and their maintenance tools during the industrial revolution led to the rapid growth of Mechanical Engineering both in its birthplace <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Britain</a> and abroad.<sup id="cite_ref-ECPD_Definition_on_Britannica_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ECPD_Definition_on_Britannica-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Chemical_Engineering" title="Chemical Engineering" class="mw-redirect">Chemical Engineering</a>, like its counterpart Mechanical Engineering, developed in the nineteenth century during the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Revolution" title="Industrial Revolution">Industrial Revolution</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ECPD_Definition_on_Britannica_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ECPD_Definition_on_Britannica-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Industrial scale manufacturing demanded new materials and new processes and by 1880 the need for large scale production of chemicals was such that a new industry was created, dedicated to the development and large scale manufacturing of chemicals in new industrial plants.<sup id="cite_ref-ECPD_Definition_on_Britannica_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ECPD_Definition_on_Britannica-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> The role of the chemical engineer was the design of these chemical plants and processes.<sup id="cite_ref-ECPD_Definition_on_Britannica_3-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ECPD_Definition_on_Britannica-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Aeronautical Engineering deals with <a href="/wiki/Aircraft" title="Aircraft">aircraft</a> design while <a href="/wiki/Aerospace_Engineering" title="Aerospace Engineering" class="mw-redirect">Aerospace Engineering</a> is a more modern term that expands the reach envelope of the discipline by including <a href="/wiki/Spacecraft" title="Spacecraft">spacecraft</a> design.<sup id="cite_ref-Imperial_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Imperial-13"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> Its origins can be traced back to the aviation pioneers around the turn of the century from the 19th century to the 20th although the work of <a href="/wiki/Sir_George_Cayley" title="Sir George Cayley" class="mw-redirect">Sir George Cayley</a> has recently been dated as being from the last decade of the 18th century. Early knowledge of aeronautical engineering was largely empirical with some concepts and skills imported from other branches of engineering.<sup id="cite_ref-americana_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-americana-14"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The first <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">PhD</a> in engineering (technically, <i>applied science and engineering</i>) awarded in the United States went to <a href="/wiki/Willard_Gibbs" title="Willard Gibbs" class="mw-redirect">Willard Gibbs</a> at <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a> in 1863; it was also the second PhD awarded in science in the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Only a decade after the successful flights by the <a href="/wiki/Wright_brothers" title="Wright brothers">Wright brothers</a>, the 1920s saw extensive development of aeronautical engineering through development of <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> military aircraft. Meanwhile, research to provide fundamental background science continued by combining <a href="/wiki/Theoretical_physics" title="Theoretical physics">theoretical physics</a> with experiments.</p>
<p>In 1990, with the rise of <a href="/wiki/Computer" title="Computer">computer</a> technology, the first <a href="/wiki/Search_engine_(computing)" title="Search engine (computing)">search engine</a> was built by <a href="/wiki/Computer_engineer" title="Computer engineer" class="mw-redirect">computer engineer</a> <a href="/wiki/Alan_Emtage" title="Alan Emtage">Alan Emtage</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Main branches of engineering">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Main_branches_of_engineering">Main branches of engineering</span></h2>
<div class="rellink relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_engineering_branches" title="List of engineering branches">List of engineering branches</a></div>
<p>Engineering, much like other science, is a broad discipline which is often broken down into several sub-disciplines. These disciplines concern themselves with differing areas of engineering work. Although initially an engineer will usually be trained in a specific discipline, throughout an engineer's career the engineer may become multi-disciplined, having worked in several of the outlined areas. Historically the main Branches of Engineering are categorized as follows:<sup id="cite_ref-Imperial_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Imperial-13"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Edinburgh_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Edinburgh-16"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Aerospace_engineering" title="Aerospace engineering">Aerospace engineering</a> - The design of aircraft, spacecraft and related topics.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Chemical_engineering" title="Chemical engineering">Chemical engineering</a> - The exploitation of chemical principles in order to carry out large scale chemical process, as well as designing new specialty <a href="/wiki/Materials_science" title="Materials science">materials</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fuel" title="Fuel">fuels</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Civil_engineering" title="Civil engineering">Civil engineering</a> - The design and construction of public and private works, such as <a href="/wiki/Infrastructure" title="Infrastructure">infrastructure</a> (<a href="/wiki/Roads" title="Roads" class="mw-redirect">roads</a>, <a href="/wiki/Railways" title="Railways" class="mw-redirect">railways</a>, water supply and treatment etc.), <a href="/wiki/Bridge" title="Bridge">bridges</a> and buildings.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Electrical_engineering" title="Electrical engineering">Electrical engineering</a> - a very broad area that may encompass the design and study of various electrical &amp; electronic systems, such as <a href="/wiki/Electrical_circuit" title="Electrical circuit" class="mw-redirect">electrical circuits</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electrical_generator" title="Electrical generator">generators</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electric_motor" title="Electric motor">motors</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electromagnetic" title="Electromagnetic">electromagnetic</a>/<a href="/wiki/Electromechanical" title="Electromechanical" class="mw-redirect">electromechanical</a> devices, <a href="/wiki/Electronic_devices" title="Electronic devices" class="mw-redirect">electronic devices</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electronic_circuits" title="Electronic circuits" class="mw-redirect">electronic circuits</a>, <a href="/wiki/Optical_fiber" title="Optical fiber">optical fibers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Optoelectronic_device" title="Optoelectronic device" class="mw-redirect">optoelectronic devices</a>, <a href="/wiki/Computer" title="Computer">computer</a> systems, <a href="/wiki/Telecommunications" title="Telecommunications" class="mw-redirect">telecommunications</a> and <a href="/wiki/Electronics" title="Electronics">electronics</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mechanical_engineering" title="Mechanical engineering">Mechanical engineering</a> - The design of physical or mechanical systems, such as <a href="/wiki/Internal_combustion_engine" title="Internal combustion engine">engines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gas_compressor" title="Gas compressor">compressors</a>, <a href="/wiki/Powertrain" title="Powertrain">powertrains</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kinematic_chain" title="Kinematic chain">kinematic chains</a>, vacuum technology, and <a href="/wiki/Vibration_isolation" title="Vibration isolation">vibration isolation</a> equipment.</li>
</ul>
<p>New specialties sometimes combine with the traditional fields and form new branches. A new or emerging area of application will commonly be defined temporarily as a permutation or subset of existing disciplines; there is often gray area as to when a given sub-field becomes large and/or prominent enough to warrant classification as a new "branch." One key indicator of such emergence is when major universities start establishing departments and programs in the new field.</p>
<p>For each of these fields there exists considerable overlap, especially in the areas of the application of sciences to their disciplines such as physics, chemistry and mathematics.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Methodology">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Methodology">Methodology</span></h2>

Design of a <a href="/wiki/Turbine" title="Turbine">turbine</a> requires collaboration of engineers from many fields, as the system is subject to mechanical, electro-magnetic and chemical processes. The <a href="/wiki/Turbine_blade" title="Turbine blade">blades</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stator" title="Stator">rotor and stator</a> as well as the <a href="/wiki/Steam_cycle" title="Steam cycle" class="mw-redirect">steam cycle</a> all need to be carefully designed and optimised.</div>
</div>

<p>Engineers apply the sciences of physics and mathematics to find suitable solutions to problems or to make improvements to the status quo. More than ever, engineers are now required to have knowledge of relevant sciences for their design projects, as a result, they keep on learning new material throughout their career.</p>
<p>If multiple options exist, engineers weigh different design choices on their merits and choose the solution that best matches the requirements. The crucial and unique task of the engineer is to identify, understand, and interpret the constraints on a design in order to produce a successful result. It is usually not enough to build a technically successful product; it must also meet further requirements.</p>
<p>Constraints may include available resources, physical, imaginative or technical limitations, flexibility for future modifications and additions, and other factors, such as requirements for cost, <a href="/wiki/Safety_engineering" title="Safety engineering">safety</a>, marketability, productibility, and <a href="/wiki/Serviceability_(computer)" title="Serviceability (computer)">serviceability</a>. By understanding the constraints, engineers derive <a href="/wiki/Specifications" title="Specifications" class="mw-redirect">specifications</a> for the limits within which a viable object or system may be produced and operated.</p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Problem solving">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Problem_solving">Problem solving</span></h3>
<p>Engineers use their knowledge of <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">science</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Logic" title="Logic">logic</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Empirical_knowledge" title="Empirical knowledge" class="mw-redirect">appropriate experience</a> to find suitable solutions to a problem. Engineering is considered a branch of applied mathematics and science. Creating an appropriate <a href="/wiki/Mathematical_model" title="Mathematical model">mathematical model</a> of a problem allows them to analyze it (sometimes definitively), and to test potential solutions.</p>
<p>Usually multiple reasonable solutions exist, so engineers must evaluate the different <a href="/wiki/Design_choice" title="Design choice">design choices</a> on their merits and choose the solution that best meets their requirements. <a href="/wiki/Genrich_Altshuller" title="Genrich Altshuller">Genrich Altshuller</a>, after gathering statistics on a large number of <a href="/wiki/Patent" title="Patent">patents</a>, suggested that <a href="/wiki/Compromise" title="Compromise">compromises</a> are at the heart of "<a href="/wiki/Level_of_invention" title="Level of invention" class="mw-redirect">low-level</a>" engineering designs, while at a higher level the best design is one which eliminates the core contradiction causing the problem.</p>
<p>Engineers typically attempt to predict how well their designs will perform to their specifications prior to full-scale production. They use, among other things: <a href="/wiki/Prototype" title="Prototype">prototypes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scale_model" title="Scale model">scale models</a>, <a href="/wiki/Simulation" title="Simulation">simulations</a>, <a href="/wiki/Destructive_testing" title="Destructive testing">destructive tests</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nondestructive_testing" title="Nondestructive testing">nondestructive tests</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Stress_test" title="Stress test">stress tests</a>. Testing ensures that products will perform as expected.</p>
<p>Engineers as professionals take seriously their responsibility to produce designs that will perform as expected and will not cause unintended harm to the public at large. Engineers typically include a <a href="/wiki/Factor_of_safety" title="Factor of safety">factor of safety</a> in their designs to reduce the risk of unexpected failure. However, the greater the safety factor, the less efficient the design may be.</p>
<p>The study of failed products is known as <a href="/wiki/Forensic_engineering" title="Forensic engineering">forensic engineering</a>, and can help the <a href="/wiki/Product_design" title="Product design">product designer</a> in evaluating his or her design in the light of real conditions. The discipline is of greatest value after disasters, such as <a href="/wiki/Bridge_collapse" title="Bridge collapse" class="mw-redirect">bridge collapses</a>, when careful analysis is needed to establish the cause or causes of the failure.</p>

<p>As with all modern scientific and technological endeavors, computers and software play an increasingly important role. As well as the typical business <a href="/wiki/Application_software" title="Application software">application software</a> there are a number of computer aided applications (<a href="/wiki/CAx" title="CAx" class="mw-redirect">Computer-aided technologies</a>) specifically for engineering. Computers can be used to generate models of fundamental physical processes, which can be solved using <a href="/wiki/Numerical_method" title="Numerical method" class="mw-redirect">numerical methods</a>.</p>
<p>One of the most widely used tools in the profession is <a href="/wiki/Computer-aided_design" title="Computer-aided design">computer-aided design</a> (CAD) software which enables engineers to create 3D models, 2D drawings, and schematics of their designs. CAD together with <a href="/wiki/Digital_mockup" title="Digital mockup">Digital mockup</a> (DMU) and <a href="/wiki/Computer-aided_engineering" title="Computer-aided engineering">CAE</a> software such as <a href="/wiki/Finite_element_method" title="Finite element method">finite element method analysis</a> or <a href="/wiki/Analytic_element_method" title="Analytic element method">analytic element method</a> allows engineers to create models of designs that can be analyzed without having to make expensive and time-consuming physical prototypes.</p>
<p>These allow products and components to be checked for flaws; assess fit and assembly; study ergonomics; and to analyze static and dynamic characteristics of systems such as stresses, temperatures, electromagnetic emissions, electrical currents and voltages, digital logic levels, fluid flows, and kinematics. Access and distribution of all this information is generally organized with the use of <a href="/wiki/Product_Data_Management" title="Product Data Management" class="mw-redirect">Product Data Management</a> software.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>There are also many tools to support specific engineering tasks such as <a href="/wiki/Computer-aided_manufacture" title="Computer-aided manufacture" class="mw-redirect">Computer-aided manufacture</a> (CAM) software to generate <a href="/wiki/CNC" title="CNC" class="mw-redirect">CNC</a> machining instructions; <a href="/wiki/Manufacturing_Process_Management" title="Manufacturing Process Management" class="mw-redirect">Manufacturing Process Management</a> software for production engineering; <a href="/wiki/Electronic_design_automation" title="Electronic design automation">EDA</a> for <a href="/wiki/Printed_circuit_board" title="Printed circuit board">printed circuit board</a> (PCB) and circuit <a href="/wiki/Schematic" title="Schematic">schematics</a> for electronic engineers; <a href="/wiki/Maintenance,_repair_and_operations" title="Maintenance, repair and operations" class="mw-redirect">MRO</a> applications for maintenance management; and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Architecture,_engineering_and_construction&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Architecture, engineering and construction (page does not exist)">AEC</a> software for civil engineering.</p>
<p>In recent years the use of computer software to aid the development of goods has collectively come to be known as <a href="/wiki/Product_Lifecycle_Management" title="Product Lifecycle Management" class="mw-redirect">Product Lifecycle Management</a> (PLM).<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>Engineering is a subject that ranges from large collaborations to small individual projects. Almost all engineering projects are beholden to some sort of financing agency: a company, a set of investors, or a government. The few types of engineering that are minimally constrained by such issues are <a href="/wiki/Pro_bono" title="Pro bono">pro bono</a> engineering and <a href="/wiki/Open_design" title="Open design">open design</a> engineering.</p>
<p>By its very nature engineering is bound up with society and human behavior. Every product or construction used by modern society will have been influenced by engineering design. Engineering design is a very powerful tool to make changes to environment, society and economies, and its application brings with it a great responsibility. Many <a href="/wiki/Engineering_society" title="Engineering society" class="mw-redirect">engineering societies</a> have established codes of practice and <a href="/wiki/Engineering_ethics" title="Engineering ethics">codes of ethics</a> to guide members and inform the public at large.</p>
<p>Engineering projects can be subject to controversy. Examples from different engineering disciplines include the development of <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_weapon" title="Nuclear weapon">nuclear weapons</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Three_Gorges_Dam" title="Three Gorges Dam">Three Gorges Dam</a>, the design and use of <a href="/wiki/Sport_utility_vehicle" title="Sport utility vehicle">Sport utility vehicles</a> and the extraction of <a href="/wiki/Fuel_oil" title="Fuel oil">oil</a>. In response, some western engineering companies have enacted serious <a href="/wiki/Corporate_social_responsibility" title="Corporate social responsibility">corporate and social responsibility</a> policies.</p>
<p>Engineering is a key driver of human development.<sup id="cite_ref-Human_Dev_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Human_Dev-19"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> Sub-Saharan Africa in particular has a very small engineering capacity which results in many African nations being unable to develop crucial infrastructure without outside aid. The attainment of many of the <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Development_Goals" title="Millennium Development Goals">Millennium Development Goals</a> requires the achievement of sufficient engineering capacity to develop infrastructure and sustainable technological development.<sup id="cite_ref-MDG_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MDG-20"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>All overseas development and relief NGOs make considerable use of engineers to apply solutions in disaster and development scenarios. A number of charitable organizations aim to use engineering directly for the good of mankind:</p>

<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nrc-bri-bioprocess-lr.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="http://bits.wikimedia.org/skins-1.5/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
Bioreactors for producing proteins, NRC Biotechnology Research Institute, Montréal, Canada</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>There exists an overlap between the sciences and engineering practice; in engineering, one applies science. Both areas of endeavor rely on accurate observation of <a href="/wiki/Materials" title="Materials" class="mw-redirect">materials</a> and phenomena. Both use mathematics and classification criteria to analyze and communicate observations.</p>
<p>Scientists are expected to interpret their observations and to make expert recommendations for practical action based on those interpretations<sup class="Template-Fact" title="This claim needs references to reliable sources from March 2007" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">citation needed</a></i>]</sup>. Scientists may also have to complete engineering tasks, such as designing experimental apparatus or building prototypes. Conversely, in the process of developing technology engineers sometimes find themselves exploring new phenomena, thus becoming, for the moment, scientists.</p>
<p>In the book <i>What Engineers Know and How They Know It</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-vincenti_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vincenti-21"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup> Walter Vincenti asserts that engineering research has a character different from that of scientific research. First, it often deals with areas in which the basic <a href="/wiki/Physics" title="Physics">physics</a> and/or <a href="/wiki/Chemistry" title="Chemistry">chemistry</a> are well understood, but the problems themselves are too complex to solve in an exact manner.</p>
<p>Examples are the use of numerical approximations to the <a href="/wiki/Navier-Stokes_equations" title="Navier-Stokes equations" class="mw-redirect">Navier-Stokes equations</a> to describe aerodynamic flow over an aircraft, or the use of <a href="/wiki/Metal_fatigue" title="Metal fatigue" class="mw-redirect">Miner's rule</a> to calculate fatigue damage. Second, engineering research employs many semi-<a href="/wiki/Empirical_methods" title="Empirical methods" class="mw-redirect">empirical methods</a> that are foreign to pure scientific research, one example being the <a href="/wiki/Method_of_variation_of_parameters" title="Method of variation of parameters" class="mw-redirect">method of parameter variation</a>.</p>
<p>As stated by Fung et al. in the revision to the classic engineering text, Foundations of <a href="/wiki/Solid_Mechanics" title="Solid Mechanics" class="mw-redirect">Solid Mechanics</a>:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"Engineering is quite different from science. Scientists try to understand nature. Engineers try to make things that do not exist in nature. Engineers stress invention. To embody an invention the engineer must put his idea in concrete terms, and design something that people can use. That something can be a device, a gadget, a material, a method, a computing program, an innovative experiment, a new solution to a problem, or an improvement on what is existing. Since a design has to be concrete, it must have its geometry, dimensions, and characteristic numbers. Almost all engineers working on new designs find that they do not have all the needed information. Most often, they are limited by insufficient scientific knowledge. Thus they study mathematics, physics, chemistry, biology and mechanics. Often they have to add to the sciences relevant to their profession. Thus engineering sciences are born."<sup id="cite_ref-Fung_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fung-22"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Although engineering solutions make use of scientific principles, engineers must also take into account safety, efficiency, economy, reliability and constructibility or ease of fabrication, as well as legal considerations such as patent infringement or liability in the case of failure of the solution.</p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Medicine and biology">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Medicine_and_biology">Medicine and biology</span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:227px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Leonardo_self.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Leonardo_self.jpg/225px-Leonardo_self.jpg" width="225" height="353" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Leonardo_self.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="http://bits.wikimedia.org/skins-1.5/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Leonardo_da_Vinci" title="Leonardo da Vinci">Leonardo da Vinci</a>, seen here in a self-portrait, has been described as the epitome of the artist/engineer.<sup id="cite_ref-Bjerklie.2C_David_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bjerklie.2C_David-23"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup> He is also known for his studies on <a href="/wiki/Human_anatomy" title="Human anatomy">human anatomy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Physiognomy" title="Physiognomy">physiognomy</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The study of the human body, albeit from different directions and for different purposes, is an important common link between medicine and some engineering disciplines. <a href="/wiki/Medicine" title="Medicine">Medicine</a> aims to sustain, enhance and even replace functions of the <a href="/wiki/Human_body" title="Human body">human body</a>, if necessary, through the use of <a href="/wiki/Technology" title="Technology">technology</a>.</p>
<p>Modern medicine can replace several of the body's functions through the use of artificial organs and can significantly alter the function of the human body through artificial devices such as, for example, <a href="/wiki/Brain_implant" title="Brain implant">brain implants</a> and <a href="/wiki/Artificial_pacemaker" title="Artificial pacemaker">pacemakers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Boston_U_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Boston_U-24"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-IEEE_foreign_parts_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IEEE_foreign_parts-25"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup> The fields of <a href="/wiki/Bionics" title="Bionics">Bionics</a> and medical Bionics are dedicated to the study of synthetic implants pertaining to natural systems.</p>
<p>Conversely, some engineering disciplines view the human body as a biological machine worth studying, and are dedicated to emulating many of its functions by replacing <a href="/wiki/Biology" title="Biology">biology</a> with technology. This has led to fields such as <a href="/wiki/Artificial_intelligence" title="Artificial intelligence">artificial intelligence</a>, <a href="/wiki/Neural_networks" title="Neural networks" class="mw-redirect">neural networks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fuzzy_logic" title="Fuzzy logic">fuzzy logic</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Robot" title="Robot">robotics</a>. There are also substantial interdisciplinary interactions between engineering and medicine.<sup id="cite_ref-IME_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IME-26"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-IEEE_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IEEE-27"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Both fields provide solutions to real world problems. This often requires moving forward before phenomena are completely understood in a more rigorous scientific sense and therefore experimentation and <a href="/wiki/Empirical" title="Empirical">empirical</a> knowledge is an integral part of both.</p>
<p>Medicine, in part, studies the function of the human body. The human body, as a biological machine, has many functions that can be modeled using Engineering methods.<sup id="cite_ref-Royal_Academy_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Royal_Academy-28"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The heart for example functions much like a pump,<sup id="cite_ref-Science_Museum_of_Minnesota_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Science_Museum_of_Minnesota-29"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup> the skeleton is like a linked structure with levers,<sup id="cite_ref-Minnesota_State_University_emuseum_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Minnesota_State_University_emuseum-30"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup> the brain produces <a href="/wiki/Signal_(electrical_engineering)" title="Signal (electrical engineering)" class="mw-redirect">electrical signals</a> etc.<sup id="cite_ref-UC_Berkeley_News_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UC_Berkeley_News-31"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup> These similarities as well as the increasing importance and application of Engineering principles in Medicine, led to the development of the field of <a href="/wiki/Biomedical_engineering" title="Biomedical engineering">biomedical engineering</a> that uses concepts developed in both disciplines.</p>
<p>Newly emerging branches of science, such as <a href="/wiki/Systems_biology" title="Systems biology">Systems biology</a>, are adapting analytical tools traditionally used for engineering, such as systems modeling and computational analysis, to the description of biological systems.<sup id="cite_ref-Royal_Academy_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Royal_Academy-28"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Art">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Art">Art</span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Booster-Layout.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Booster-Layout.jpg/200px-Booster-Layout.jpg" width="200" height="71" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Booster-Layout.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="http://bits.wikimedia.org/skins-1.5/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
A drawing for a booster engine for <a href="/wiki/Steam_locomotive" title="Steam locomotive">steam locomotives</a>. Engineering is applied to <a href="/wiki/Design" title="Design">design</a>, with emphasis on function and the utilization of mathematics and science.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>There are connections between engineering and art;<sup id="cite_ref-Lehigh_University_project_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lehigh_University_project-32"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup> they are direct in some fields, for example, <a href="/wiki/Architecture" title="Architecture">architecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Landscape_architecture" title="Landscape architecture">landscape architecture</a> and <a href="/wiki/Industrial_design" title="Industrial design">industrial design</a> (even to the extent that these disciplines may sometimes be included in a University's <a href="/wiki/Faculty_(university)" title="Faculty (university)">Faculty</a> of Engineering); and indirect in others.<sup id="cite_ref-Lehigh_University_project_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lehigh_University_project-32"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-National_Science_Foundation:The_Art_of_Engineering_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-National_Science_Foundation:The_Art_of_Engineering-33"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MIT_World:The_Art_of_Engineering_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MIT_World:The_Art_of_Engineering-34"><span>[</span>35<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-University_of_Texas_at_Dallas_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-University_of_Texas_at_Dallas-35"><span>[</span>36<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Art_Institute_of_Chicago" title="Art Institute of Chicago">Art Institute of Chicago</a>, for instance, held an exhibition about the art of <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a>'s aerospace design.<sup id="cite_ref-NASA_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NASA-36"><span>[</span>37<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Robert_Maillart" title="Robert Maillart">Robert Maillart</a>'s bridge design is perceived by some to have been deliberately artistic.<sup id="cite_ref-Princeton_U_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Princeton_U-37"><span>[</span>38<span>]</span></a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/University_of_South_Florida" title="University of South Florida">University of South Florida</a>, an engineering professor, through a grant with the <a href="/wiki/National_Science_Foundation" title="National Science Foundation">National Science Foundation</a>, has developed a course that connects art and engineering.<sup id="cite_ref-National_Science_Foundation:The_Art_of_Engineering_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-National_Science_Foundation:The_Art_of_Engineering-33"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Chief_engineer_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chief_engineer-38"><span>[</span>39<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Among famous historical figures <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_Da_Vinci" title="Leonardo Da Vinci" class="mw-redirect">Leonardo Da Vinci</a> is a well known <a href="/wiki/Renaissance" title="Renaissance">Renaissance</a> artist and engineer, and a prime example of the nexus between art and engineering.<sup id="cite_ref-Bjerklie.2C_David_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bjerklie.2C_David-23"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Drew_U_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Drew_U-39"><span>[</span>40<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Other fields">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Other_fields">Other fields</span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Political_science" title="Political science">Political science</a> the term <i>engineering</i> has been borrowed for the study of the subjects of <a href="/wiki/Social_engineering_(political_science)" title="Social engineering (political science)">Social engineering</a> and <a href="/wiki/Political_engineering" title="Political engineering">Political engineering</a>, which deal with forming <a href="/wiki/Political_structure" title="Political structure">political</a> and <a href="/wiki/Social_structure" title="Social structure">social structures</a> using engineering methodology coupled with <a href="/wiki/Political_science" title="Political science">political science</a> principles.</p>
