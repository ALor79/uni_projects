<p><b>History of the world</b> (or "World History") is generally taken to encompass the history of the human race (<i><a href="/wiki/Homo_sapiens" title="Homo sapiens" class="mw-redirect">Homo sapiens</a></i>), from the earliest times to the present, in all places on earth. By convention, it generally excludes non-human <i><a href="/wiki/Natural_History" title="Natural History" class="mw-redirect">natural history</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Quaternary" title="Quaternary">geological history</a></i>, except insofar as the natural world substantially affects human lives. It encompasses the study of written records, from ancient times forward, plus additional knowledge gained from other sources, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Archaeology" title="Archaeology">archaeology</a></i>. <a href="/wiki/Ancient" title="Ancient" class="mw-redirect">Ancient</a> recorded <a href="/wiki/History" title="History">history</a><sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> begins with the invention, independently at several sites on Earth, of <a href="/wiki/Writing" title="Writing">writing</a>, which created the infrastructure for lasting, accurately transmitted memories and thus for the diffusion and growth of <a href="/wiki/Knowledge" title="Knowledge">knowledge</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-WebsterWH_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WebsterWH-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> However, the roots of <a href="/wiki/Civilization" title="Civilization">civilization</a> reach back to the period before writing — humanity's <a href="/wiki/Prehistory" title="Prehistory">prehistory</a>.</p>
<p>Human prehistory begins in the <a href="/wiki/Paleolithic" title="Paleolithic">Paleolithic</a>, or Early Stone Age. During the Neolithic (New Stone Age) <a href="/wiki/Neolithic_Revolution" title="Neolithic Revolution">Agricultural Revolution</a> between 8,500 and 7,000 BCE in the <a href="/wiki/Fertile_Crescent" title="Fertile Crescent">Fertile Crescent</a>, humans began the systematic husbandry of plants and animals&#160;— <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Tudge_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tudge-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> It spread to neighboring regions, and also developed independently elsewhere, until most humans lived as farmers in permanent settlements.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> The relative security and increased productivity provided by farming allowed these communities to expand. They grew over time into increasingly larger units in parallel with the evolution of ever more efficient means of <a href="/wiki/Transport" title="Transport">transport</a>.</p>
<p>Surplus food made possible an increasing <a href="/wiki/Division_of_labour" title="Division of labour">division of labor</a>, the rise of a leisured <a href="/wiki/Upper_class" title="Upper class">upper class</a>, and the development of <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">cities</a> and thus of <a href="/wiki/Civilization" title="Civilization">civilization</a>. The growing complexity of human societies necessitated systems of <a href="/wiki/Accounting" title="Accounting" class="mw-redirect">accounting</a>, which led to <a href="/wiki/Writing" title="Writing">writing</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DSB-AO_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DSB-AO-7"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Civilizations developed on the banks of life-sustaining bodies of fresh <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a> (lakes and <a href="/wiki/River" title="River">rivers</a>). By 3,000 BCE they had arisen in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Mesopotamia" title="Mesopotamia">Mesopotamia</a> (the "land between the Rivers" <a href="/wiki/Euphrates" title="Euphrates">Euphrates</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tigris" title="Tigris">Tigris</a>),<sup id="cite_ref-McNeill-Sumer_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McNeill-Sumer-8"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> on the banks of <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Egypt</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Nile_River" title="Nile River" class="mw-redirect">River Nile</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-grimal1992_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grimal1992-11"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> and in the <a href="/wiki/Indus_Valley_Civilization" title="Indus Valley Civilization">Indus River valley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> Similar civilizations are believed also to have arisen at this time along the great rivers of <a href="/wiki/Three_Sovereigns_and_Five_Emperors" title="Three Sovereigns and Five Emperors">China</a>, but the archaeological evidence for extensive urban construction is less distinct.</p>
<p>The history of the <a href="/wiki/Old_World" title="Old World">Old World</a> (<a href="/wiki/History_of_Europe" title="History of Europe">Europe</a> in particular) is commonly divided into <a href="/wiki/Ancient_history" title="Ancient history">Antiquity</a>, up to 476 CE; the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> from the 5th through the 15th centuries, including the early European <a href="/wiki/Renaissance" title="Renaissance">Renaissance</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Early_Modern_period" title="Early Modern period" class="mw-redirect">Early Modern period</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-rice1970_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rice1970-17"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> from the 15th century to the late 18th, including the <a href="/wiki/Age_of_Enlightenment" title="Age of Enlightenment">Age of Enlightenment</a>; and the <a href="/wiki/Modern_period" title="Modern period" class="mw-redirect">Modern period</a>, from the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Revolution" title="Industrial Revolution">Industrial Revolution</a> to the present.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, the fall of the <a href="/wiki/Western_Roman_Empire" title="Western Roman Empire">Western Roman Empire</a> (476 CE) is commonly taken as signaling the end of <a href="/wiki/Ancient_history" title="Ancient history">antiquity</a> and the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a>, during which (around the year 1300) the <a href="/wiki/European_Renaissance" title="European Renaissance" class="mw-redirect">European Renaissance</a><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> emerges. In the mid-15th century, <a href="/wiki/Johannes_Gutenberg" title="Johannes Gutenberg">Johannes Gutenberg</a>'s invention of modern <a href="/wiki/Printing" title="Printing">printing</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> employing <a href="/wiki/Movable_type" title="Movable type">movable type</a>, revolutionized <a href="/wiki/Communication" title="Communication">communication</a>, helping end the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a> and usher in modern times and the <a href="/wiki/Scientific_Revolution" title="Scientific Revolution" class="mw-redirect">Scientific Revolution</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup> By the 18th century, the accumulation of <a href="/wiki/Knowledge" title="Knowledge">knowledge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Technology" title="Technology">technology</a>, especially in Europe, had reached a <a href="/wiki/Critical_mass" title="Critical mass">critical mass</a> that brought about the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Revolution" title="Industrial Revolution">Industrial Revolution</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In other parts of the world, in the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Near_East" title="Ancient Near East">ancient Near East</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ancient_China" title="Ancient China" class="mw-redirect">ancient China</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/History_of_India" title="History of India">ancient India</a> and elsewhere, the historic timeline unfolded differently, but by the 18th century, due to extensive world trade and colonization, the histories of the many human civilizations had in large measure converged. In the last quarter-<a href="/wiki/Millennium" title="Millennium">millennium</a>, the growth of <a href="/wiki/Knowledge" title="Knowledge">knowledge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Technology" title="Technology">technology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Commerce" title="Commerce">commerce</a>, and of the potential destructiveness of <a href="/wiki/War" title="War">war</a> has accelerated, creating the <a href="/wiki/Opportunity" title="Opportunity">opportunities</a> and perils that now confront the many human communities that inhabit the <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">planet</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_the_world&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Prehistory">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Prehistory">Prehistory</span></h2>
<div class="rellink relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Paleolithic" title="Paleolithic">Paleolithic</a></div>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Homo_sapiens" title="Homo sapiens" class="mw-redirect">Homo sapiens</a></i> first arose on the <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a> between 400,000 and 250,000 years ago during the <a href="/wiki/Palaeolithic" title="Palaeolithic" class="mw-redirect">Palaeolithic</a> period. This occurred after a long period of <a href="/wiki/Human_evolution" title="Human evolution">evolution</a>. Ancestors of humans, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Homo_erectus" title="Homo erectus">Homo erectus</a></i>, had been using simple tools for many millennia, but as time progressed, tools became far more refined and complex. At some point, humans had begun using <a href="/wiki/Fire" title="Fire">fire</a> for <a href="/wiki/Heat" title="Heat">heat</a> and for <a href="/wiki/Cooking" title="Cooking">cooking</a>. Humans also developed <a href="/wiki/Language" title="Language">language</a> in the Palaeolithic, as well as a conceptual repertoire that included systematic burial of the dead and adornment of the living. During this period, all humans lived as <a href="/wiki/Hunter-gatherer" title="Hunter-gatherer">hunter-gatherers</a>, who were generally <a href="/wiki/Nomadic" title="Nomadic" class="mw-redirect">nomadic</a>.</p>
<p>Modern humans spread rapidly from <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> and the frost-free zones of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. The rapid expansion of humankind to <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oceania" title="Oceania">Oceania</a> took place at the climax of the most recent <a href="/wiki/Ice_Age" title="Ice Age" class="mw-redirect">Ice Age</a>, when temperate regions of today were extremely inhospitable. Yet, humans had colonised nearly all the ice-free parts of the globe by the end of the Ice Age, some 12,000 years ago.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Neolithic_Revolution" title="Neolithic Revolution">Agricultural Revolution</a>, beginning about 10,000 BCE, saw the development of agriculture. Farming permitted far denser populations, which in time organised into <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_state" title="Sovereign state">states</a>. Agriculture also created <a href="/wiki/Food" title="Food">food</a> surpluses that could support people not directly engaged in food production. The development of agriculture permitted the creation of the first <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">cities</a>. These were centres of <a href="/wiki/Trade" title="Trade">trade</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manufacture" title="Manufacture" class="mw-redirect">manufacture</a> and <a href="/wiki/Political_power" title="Political power">political power</a> with nearly no agricultural production of their own. Cities established a <a href="/wiki/Symbiosis" title="Symbiosis">symbiosis</a> with their surrounding <a href="/wiki/Countryside" title="Countryside" class="mw-redirect">countrysides</a>, absorbing agricultural products and providing, in return, manufactures and varying degrees of military control and protection.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>The development of cities equated, both <a href="/wiki/Etymologically" title="Etymologically" class="mw-redirect">etymologically</a> and in fact, with the rise of <a href="/wiki/Civilization" title="Civilization">civilization</a> itself. In about 40,000 BC, before the age of cities, there is evidence of people living in man-made shelter huts in northern Punjab and central Asia (Bactria). By 7,000 BC, there is evidence of people growing barley in this area, and raising sheep and goats. Around this time, people begin living in mud-brick dwellings in villages, some of which are still in existence. Early cities arose in the first <a href="/wiki/Sumer" title="Sumer">Sumerian civilization</a>, in lower <a href="/wiki/Mesopotamia" title="Mesopotamia">Mesopotamia</a> (3500 BCE),<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup> followed by <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Egyptian civilization</a> along the <a href="/wiki/Nile" title="Nile">Nile</a> (3300 BCE)<sup id="cite_ref-grimal1992_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grimal1992-11"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Indus_Valley_Civilization" title="Indus Valley Civilization">Harappan civilization</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Indus_River" title="Indus River">Indus Valley</a> (3300 BCE).<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span>[</span>35<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span>[</span>36<span>]</span></a></sup> Elaborate cities grew up, with high levels of social and economic complexity. Each of these civilizations was so different from the others that they almost certainly originated independently. It was at this time, and due to the needs of cities, that <a href="/wiki/Writing" title="Writing">writing</a> and extensive <a href="/wiki/Trade" title="Trade">trade</a> were introduced.</p>
<p>This period also saw the origins of complex <a href="/wiki/Religion" title="Religion">religion</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span>[</span>37<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span>[</span>38<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38"><span>[</span>39<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Religious_belief" title="Religious belief">Religious belief</a> in this period commonly consisted in the worship of a <a href="/wiki/Mother_Goddess" title="Mother Goddess" class="mw-redirect">Mother Goddess</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Sky_Father" title="Sky Father" class="mw-redirect">Sky Father</a>, and of the <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">Sun</a> and <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">Moon</a> as deities.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39"><span>[</span>40<span>]</span></a></sup> (See also: <a href="/wiki/Sun_worship" title="Sun worship" class="mw-redirect">Sun worship</a>.) <a href="/wiki/Shrine" title="Shrine">Shrines</a> developed, which over time evolved into <a href="/wiki/Temple" title="Temple">temple</a> establishments, complete with a complex hierarchy of <a href="/wiki/Priesthood" title="Priesthood" class="mw-redirect">priests and priestesses</a> and other functionaries. Typical of the Neolithic was a tendency to worship <a href="/wiki/Anthropomorphism" title="Anthropomorphism">anthropomorphic</a> <a href="/wiki/Deities" title="Deities" class="mw-redirect">deities</a>. Some of the earliest surviving written religious scriptures are the <i><a href="/wiki/Pyramid_Texts" title="Pyramid Texts">Pyramid Texts</a></i>, produced by the Egyptians, the oldest of which date to between 2400 and 2300 BCE.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40"><span>[</span>41<span>]</span></a></sup> Some archaeologists suggest, based on ongoing excavations of a temple complex at <a href="/wiki/G%C3%B6bekli_Tepe" title="Göbekli Tepe">Göbekli Tepe</a> ("Potbelly Hill") in southern Turkey, dating from ca. 11,500 years ago, that religion predated the <a href="/wiki/Agricultural_Revolution" title="Agricultural Revolution" class="mw-redirect">Agricultural Revolution</a> rather than following in its wake, as had generally been assumed.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41"><span>[</span>42<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_the_world&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ancient history">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Ancient_history">Ancient history</span></h2>
<div class="rellink relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Ancient_history" title="Ancient history">Ancient history</a></div>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_the_world&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Origin of civilization">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Origin_of_civilization">Origin of civilization</span></h3>
<div class="rellink relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age" title="Bronze Age">Bronze Age</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Pyramids_of_Egypt1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Pyramids_of_Egypt1.jpg/220px-Pyramids_of_Egypt1.jpg" width="220" height="144" class="thumbimage" /></a>

<a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Ancient Egyptians</a> built the <a href="/wiki/Great_Pyramids_of_Giza" title="Great Pyramids of Giza" class="mw-redirect">Great Pyramids of Giza</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age" title="Bronze Age">Bronze Age</a> forms part of a <a href="/wiki/Three-age_system" title="Three-age system">three-age system</a>. In this system, in some areas of the world, the Bronze Age follows the <a href="/wiki/Neolithic" title="Neolithic">Stone Age</a>. During this era the most fertile areas of the world saw <a href="/wiki/City_state" title="City state" class="mw-redirect">city states</a> and the first civilizations develop. These were concentrated on four fertile river valleys: The <a href="/wiki/Tigris" title="Tigris">Tigris</a> and <a href="/wiki/Euphrates" title="Euphrates">Euphrates</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mesopotamia" title="Mesopotamia">Mesopotamia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Nile" title="Nile">Nile</a> in <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Indus" title="Indus" class="mw-redirect">Indus</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South Asia</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Yangtze" title="Yangtze" class="mw-redirect">Yangtze</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yellow_River" title="Yellow River">Yellow River</a> in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Mesopotamia" title="Mesopotamia">Mesopotamia</a> saw the rise of the <a href="/wiki/Sumerian" title="Sumerian">Sumerian</a> city states. It was in these cities that the earliest known form of writing, <a href="/wiki/Cuneiform_script" title="Cuneiform script">cuneiform script</a>, appeared ca. 3000 BCE. Cuneiform writing began as a system of <a href="/wiki/Pictography" title="Pictography" class="mw-redirect">pictographs</a>. Over time, the pictorial representations became simplified and more abstract. Cuneiforms were written on <a href="/wiki/Clay_tablet" title="Clay tablet">clay tablets</a>, on which <a href="/wiki/Symbol" title="Symbol">symbols</a> were drawn with a blunt <a href="/wiki/Phragmites" title="Phragmites">reed</a> for a <a href="/wiki/Stylus" title="Stylus">stylus</a>. Writing made the administration of a large state far easier. This era also saw new military technologies, such as chariots, that allowed armies to move faster.</p>
<p>These developments led to the development of <a href="/wiki/Empire" title="Empire">empires</a>. The first empire, controlling a large territory and many cities, developed in Egypt that formed with the unification of <a href="/wiki/Lower_and_Upper_Egypt" title="Lower and Upper Egypt" class="mw-redirect">Lower and Upper Egypt</a> c. 3100 BCE . Over the next millennia the other river valleys would also see monarchical empires rise to power. In the 24th century BCE, the <a href="/wiki/Akkadian_Empire" title="Akkadian Empire">Akkadian Empire</a> arose in <a href="/wiki/Mesopotamia" title="Mesopotamia">Mesopotamia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42"><span>[</span>43<span>]</span></a></sup> and in China the <a href="/wiki/Xia_Dynasty" title="Xia Dynasty">Xia Dynasty</a> arose c. 2200 BCE.</p>
<p>Over the next millennia civilizations would develop across the world. <a href="/wiki/Trade" title="Trade">Trade</a> would increasingly become a source of power as states with access to important resources or controlling important trade routes would rise to dominance. In c.2,500 BCE the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Kerma" title="Kingdom of Kerma" class="mw-redirect">Kingdom of Kerma</a> developed in <a href="/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a> south of Egypt. In modern Turkey the <a href="/wiki/Hittites" title="Hittites">Hittites</a> controlled a large empire and by 1600 BCE, <a href="/wiki/Mycenaean_Greece" title="Mycenaean Greece">Mycenaean Greece</a> began to develop.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span>[</span>44<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44"><span>[</span>45<span>]</span></a></sup> In India this era was the <a href="/wiki/Vedic_civilization" title="Vedic civilization" class="mw-redirect">Vedic period</a>, which laid the foundations of <a href="/wiki/Hinduism" title="Hinduism">Hinduism</a> and other cultural aspects of early Indian society, and ended in the 6th century BCE. From around 550 BCE, many independent kingdoms and republics known as the <a href="/wiki/Mahajanapadas" title="Mahajanapadas">Mahajanapadas</a> were established across the country. In the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a>, civilizations such as the <a href="/wiki/Maya_civilization" title="Maya civilization">Maya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zapotec_civilization" title="Zapotec civilization">Zapotec</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moche" title="Moche">Moche</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ica-Nazca_culture" title="Ica-Nazca culture" class="mw-redirect">Nazca</a> emerged in <a href="/wiki/Mesoamerica" title="Mesoamerica">Mesoamerica</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a> at the end of the 1st millennium BCE.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_the_world&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Ancient empires">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Ancient_empires">Ancient empires</span></h2>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_the_world&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Religion and philosophy">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Religion_and_philosophy">Religion and philosophy</span></h3>
<div class="rellink relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Axial_age" title="Axial age" class="mw-redirect">Axial age</a></div>
<div class="rellink relarticle mainarticle">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/History_of_philosophy" title="History of philosophy">History of philosophy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Timeline_of_religion" title="Timeline of religion">Timeline of religion</a>, and <a href="/wiki/History_of_religion" title="History of religion" class="mw-redirect">History of religion</a></div>

<p>Beginning in the 6th century BCE a set of transformative religious and philosophical ideas developed. During this century Chinese <a href="/wiki/Confucianism" title="Confucianism">Confucianism</a>, Indian <a href="/wiki/Buddhism" title="Buddhism">Buddhism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jainism" title="Jainism">Jainism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Persia" title="Persia" class="mw-redirect">Persian</a> <a href="/wiki/Zoroastrianism" title="Zoroastrianism">Zoroastrianism</a>, Ancient Egyptian <a href="/wiki/Monotheism" title="Monotheism">Monotheism</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jewish" title="Jewish" class="mw-redirect">Jewish</a> Monotheism all developed. In the 5th century <a href="/wiki/Socrates" title="Socrates">Socrates</a> and <a href="/wiki/Plato" title="Plato">Plato</a> would lay the foundations of <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek_philosophy" title="Ancient Greek philosophy">Ancient Greek philosophy</a>.</p>
<p>In the east, three schools of thought were to dominate <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">Chinese</a> thinking until the modern day. These were <a href="/wiki/Taoism" title="Taoism">Taoism</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45"><span>[</span>46<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Legalism_(philosophy)" title="Legalism (philosophy)" class="mw-redirect">Legalism</a><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46"><span>[</span>47<span>]</span></a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Confucianism" title="Confucianism">Confucianism</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47"><span>[</span>48<span>]</span></a></sup> The Confucian tradition, which would attain dominance, looked for <a href="/wiki/Political" title="Political" class="mw-redirect">political</a> <a href="/wiki/Morality" title="Morality">morality</a> not to the force of law but to the power and example of tradition. Confucianism would later spread into the <a href="/wiki/Korean_peninsula" title="Korean peninsula" class="mw-redirect">Korean peninsula</a> and toward <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>
<p>In the west, the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greece" title="Ancient Greece">Greek</a> philosophical tradition, represented by <a href="/wiki/Socrates" title="Socrates">Socrates</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-enc1911_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-enc1911-48"><span>[</span>49<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Plato" title="Plato">Plato</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49"><span>[</span>50<span>]</span></a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Aristotle" title="Aristotle">Aristotle</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50"><span>[</span>51<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51"><span>[</span>52<span>]</span></a></sup> was diffused throughout <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> in the 4th century BCE by the conquests of Alexander III of Macedon, more commonly known as <a href="/wiki/Alexander_the_Great" title="Alexander the Great">Alexander the Great</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52"><span>[</span>53<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53"><span>[</span>54<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54"><span>[</span>55<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_the_world&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Regional empires">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Regional_empires">Regional empires</span></h3>
<div class="rellink relarticle mainarticle">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Civilization" title="Civilization">Civilization</a> and <a href="/wiki/Global_empire" title="Global empire" class="mw-redirect">Global empire</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">

<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Parthenon_from_west.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="http://bits.wikimedia.org/skins-1.5/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
The <a href="/wiki/Parthenon" title="Parthenon">Parthenon</a> epitomizes the sophisticated culture of the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greece" title="Ancient Greece">ancient Greeks</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The millennia from 500 BCE to 500 CE saw a series of empires of unprecedented size develop. Well-trained professional armies, unifying ideologies, and advanced bureaucracies created the possibility for emperors to rule over domains, whose population could attain numbers upwards of tens of millions of subjects.</p>
<p>This period in the history of the world was marked by slow but steady technological advances, with important developments such as the <a href="/wiki/Stirrup" title="Stirrup">stirrup</a> and <a href="/wiki/Moldboard_plow" title="Moldboard plow" class="mw-redirect">moldboard plow</a> arriving every few centuries. There were, however, in some regions, periods of rapid technological progress. Most important, perhaps, was the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean</a> area during the <a href="/wiki/Hellenistic_period" title="Hellenistic period">Hellenistic period</a>, when hundreds of technologies were invented.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55"><span>[</span>56<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56"><span>[</span>57<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57"><span>[</span>58<span>]</span></a></sup> Such periods were followed by periods of technological decay, as during the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a>'s decline and fall and the ensuing <a href="/wiki/Early_medieval" title="Early medieval" class="mw-redirect">early medieval</a> period.</p>
<p>The great <a href="/wiki/Empire" title="Empire">empires</a> depended on <a href="/wiki/Military" title="Military">military</a> <a href="/wiki/Annexation" title="Annexation">annexation</a> of territory and on the formation of defended settlements to become agricultural centres.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58"><span>[</span>59<span>]</span></a></sup> The relative peace that the empires brought encouraged <a href="/wiki/International_trade" title="International trade">international trade</a>, most notably the massive trade routes in the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean</a> that had been developed by the time of the <a href="/wiki/Hellenistic_Age" title="Hellenistic Age" class="mw-redirect">Hellenistic Age</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Silk_Road" title="Silk Road">Silk Road</a>.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:PtolemyWorldMap.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/PtolemyWorldMap.jpg/220px-PtolemyWorldMap.jpg" width="220" height="150" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:PtolemyWorldMap.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="http://bits.wikimedia.org/skins-1.5/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Ptolemy%27s_world_map" title="Ptolemy's world map">Ptolemy's world map</a>, reconstituted from his <i>Geographia</i> (ca. 150)</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The empires faced common problems associated with maintaining huge armies and supporting a central bureaucracy. These costs fell most heavily on the <a href="/wiki/Peasant" title="Peasant">peasantry</a>, while land-owning <a href="/wiki/Magnate" title="Magnate">magnates</a> were increasingly able to evade centralised control and its costs. The pressure of <a href="/wiki/Barbarians" title="Barbarians" class="mw-redirect">barbarians</a> on the frontiers hastened the process of internal dissolution. <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Han_Empire" title="Han Empire" class="mw-redirect">Han Empire</a> fell into <a href="/wiki/Civil_war" title="Civil war">civil war</a> in 220 CE, while its <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman</a> counterpart became increasingly decentralised and divided about the same time.</p>
<p>In the west, the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greeks" title="Ancient Greeks" class="mw-redirect">Greeks</a> established a civilization that is the foundational culture of modern <a href="/wiki/Western_culture" title="Western culture">western civilization</a>. Some centuries later, in the 3rd century BCE, the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Romans</a> began expanding their territory through conquest and colonization. By the reign of Emperor <a href="/wiki/Augustus" title="Augustus">Augustus</a> (late 1st century BCE), Rome controlled all the lands surrounding the Mediterranean. By the reign of Emperor <a href="/wiki/Trajan" title="Trajan">Trajan</a> (early 2nd century CE), Rome controlled much of the land from <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> to <a href="/wiki/Mesopotamia" title="Mesopotamia">Mesopotamia</a>.</p>
<p>In the 3rd century BCE, most of <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South Asia</a> was united into the <a href="/wiki/Maurya_Empire" title="Maurya Empire">Maurya Empire</a> by <a href="/wiki/Chandragupta_Maurya" title="Chandragupta Maurya">Chandragupta Maurya</a> and flourished under <a href="/wiki/Ashoka_the_Great" title="Ashoka the Great" class="mw-redirect">Ashoka the Great</a>. From the 3rd century CE, the <a href="/wiki/Gupta_dynasty" title="Gupta dynasty" class="mw-redirect">Gupta dynasty</a> oversaw the period referred to as ancient India's Golden Age. Empires in Southern India included those of the <a href="/wiki/Chalukyas" title="Chalukyas" class="mw-redirect">Chalukyas</a>,the <a href="/wiki/Rashtrakutas" title="Rashtrakutas" class="mw-redirect">Rashtrakutas</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Hoysalas" title="Hoysalas" class="mw-redirect">Hoysalas</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Cholas" title="Cholas" class="mw-redirect">Cholas</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Vijayanagara_Empire" title="Vijayanagara Empire">Vijayanagara Empire</a>. <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">Science</a>, <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Art" title="Art">art</a>, <a href="/wiki/Literature" title="Literature">literature</a>, <a href="/wiki/Astronomy" title="Astronomy">astronomy</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Philosophy" title="Philosophy">philosophy</a> flourished under the patronage of these kings.</p>
<p>Meanwhile, the <a href="/wiki/Han_Dynasty" title="Han Dynasty">Han Dynasty</a> was the classical empire of the East. Across the silk road from the Roman Empire, the Han Dynasty is often considered to be the Rome of China. While the Romans were almost unstoppable in military means, Han China was developing advanced cartography, ship-building, and navigation. The East developed blast furnaces, and were capable of creating finely tuned copper instruments. As with other areas during the Classical Period, Han China advanced in strides in areas of government, education, mathematics, astronomy, and technology, among others.</p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_the_world&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Declines and falls">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Declines_and_falls">Declines and falls</span></h3>
<p>The great empires of Eurasia were all located on temperate coastal plains. From the <a href="/wiki/Central_Asian" title="Central Asian" class="mw-redirect">Central Asian</a> steppes, horse-based nomads dominated a large part of the continent. The development of the <a href="/wiki/Stirrup" title="Stirrup">stirrup</a>, and the breeding of horses strong enough to carry a fully armed archer, made the nomads a constant threat to the more settled civilizations.</p>
<p>The gradual break-up of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59"><span>[</span>60<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60"><span>[</span>61<span>]</span></a></sup> spanning several centuries after the 2nd century CE, coincided with the spread of <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a> westward from the Middle East. The Western Roman Empire fell<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61"><span>[</span>62<span>]</span></a></sup> under the domination of <a href="/wiki/Germanic_tribes" title="Germanic tribes" class="mw-redirect">Germanic tribes</a> in the 5th century, and these <a href="/wiki/Polities" title="Polities" class="mw-redirect">polities</a> gradually developed into a number of warring states, all associated in one way or another with the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" title="Roman Catholic Church" class="mw-redirect">Roman Catholic Church</a>. The remaining part of the Roman Empire, in the eastern Mediterranean, would henceforth be the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine Empire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62"><span>[</span>63<span>]</span></a></sup> Centuries later, a limited unity would be restored to <a href="/wiki/Western_Europe" title="Western Europe">western Europe</a> through the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Empire</a><sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63"><span>[</span>64<span>]</span></a></sup> in 962, comprising a number of states in what is now <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, and parts of <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>.</p>
<p>In China, <a href="/wiki/Dynasties" title="Dynasties" class="mw-redirect">dynasties</a> would similarly rise and fall.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64"><span>[</span>65<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65"><span>[</span>66<span>]</span></a></sup> After the fall of the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Han_Dynasty" title="Eastern Han Dynasty" class="mw-redirect">Eastern Han Dynasty</a><sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66"><span>[</span>67<span>]</span></a></sup> and the demise of the <a href="/wiki/Three_Kingdoms" title="Three Kingdoms">Three Kingdoms</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nomad" title="Nomad">Nomadic</a> tribes from the north began to invade in the 4th century CE, eventually conquering areas of Northern China and setting up many small kingdoms. The <a href="/wiki/Sui_Dynasty" title="Sui Dynasty">Sui Dynasty</a> reunified China in 581, and under the succeeding <a href="/wiki/Tang_Dynasty" title="Tang Dynasty">Tang Dynasty</a> (618-907) China entered a second <a href="/wiki/Golden_age" title="Golden age" class="mw-redirect">golden age</a>. The Tang Dynasty also splintered, however, and after <a href="/wiki/Five_Dynasties_and_Ten_Kingdoms_Period" title="Five Dynasties and Ten Kingdoms Period">half a century of turmoil</a> the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Song_Dynasty" title="Northern Song Dynasty" class="mw-redirect">Northern Song Dynasty</a> reunified China in 982. Yet pressure from nomadic empires to the north became increasingly urgent. <a href="/wiki/North_China" title="North China">North China</a> was lost to the <a href="/wiki/Jurchens" title="Jurchens" class="mw-redirect">Jurchens</a> in 1141, and the <a href="/wiki/Mongol_Empire" title="Mongol Empire">Mongol Empire</a><sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67"><span>[</span>68<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68"><span>[</span>69<span>]</span></a></sup> conquered all of China in 1279, as well as almost all of <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">Eurasia</a>'s landmass, missing only <a href="/wiki/Central_Europe" title="Central Europe">central</a> and <a href="/wiki/Western_Europe" title="Western Europe">western Europe</a>, and most of <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>
<p>In these times, northern <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> was ruled by the <a href="/wiki/Gupta_Empire" title="Gupta Empire">Guptas</a>. In southern India, three prominent <a href="/wiki/Dravidian_people" title="Dravidian people" class="mw-redirect">Dravidian</a> kingdoms emerged: <a href="/wiki/Chera_dynasty" title="Chera dynasty" class="mw-redirect">Cheras</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chola" title="Chola" class="mw-redirect">Cholas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pandyas" title="Pandyas" class="mw-redirect">Pandyas</a>. The ensuing stability contributed to heralding in the golden age of <a href="/wiki/Hindu" title="Hindu">Hindu</a> culture in the 4th and 5th centuries CE.</p>

<p>Also at this time in <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69"><span>[</span>70<span>]</span></a></sup> vast societies began to be built, the most notable being the <a href="/wiki/Maya_civilization" title="Maya civilization">Maya</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aztecs" title="Aztecs" class="mw-redirect">Aztecs</a> of <a href="/wiki/Mesoamerica" title="Mesoamerica">Mesoamerica</a>. As the <a href="/wiki/Mother_culture" title="Mother culture">mother culture</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Olmecs" title="Olmecs" class="mw-redirect">Olmecs</a><sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70"><span>[</span>71<span>]</span></a></sup> gradually declined, the great Mayan <a href="/wiki/City-state" title="City-state">city-states</a> slowly rose in number and prominence, and Maya culture spread throughout <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n" title="Yucatán">Yucatán</a> and surrounding areas. The later empire of the <a href="/wiki/Aztecs" title="Aztecs" class="mw-redirect">Aztecs</a> was built on neighboring cultures and was influenced by conquered peoples such as the <a href="/wiki/Toltec" title="Toltec">Toltecs</a>.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>, the 14th and 15th centuries saw the rise of the <a href="/wiki/Inca" title="Inca" class="mw-redirect">Inca</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Inca_Empire" title="Inca Empire">Inca Empire</a> of <a href="/wiki/Tawantinsuyu" title="Tawantinsuyu" class="mw-redirect">Tawantinsuyu</a>, with its capital at <a href="/wiki/Cusco" title="Cusco">Cusco</a>, spanned the entire <a href="/wiki/Andes" title="Andes">Andes</a> <a href="/wiki/Mountain_Range" title="Mountain Range" class="mw-redirect">Mountain Range</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71"><span>[</span>72<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72"><span>[</span>73<span>]</span></a></sup> The Inca were prosperous and advanced, known for an excellent <a href="/wiki/Inca_road_system" title="Inca road system">road system</a> and unrivaled <a href="/wiki/Masonry" title="Masonry">masonry</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_the_world&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Middle Ages">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Middle_Ages">Middle Ages</span></h2>
<div class="rellink relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a></div>
<p>The Middle Ages are commonly dated from the <a href="/wiki/Fall_of_the_Roman_Empire" title="Fall of the Roman Empire" class="mw-redirect">fall of the Western Roman Empire</a> in the 5th century.</p>
<p>The period corresponds to the <a href="/wiki/Islamic_conquests" title="Islamic conquests" class="mw-redirect">Islamic conquests</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73"><span>[</span>74<span>]</span></a></sup> subsequent <a href="/wiki/Islamic_golden_age" title="Islamic golden age" class="mw-redirect">Islamic golden age</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74"><span>[</span>75<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75"><span>[</span>76<span>]</span></a></sup> and commencement and expansion of the Islamic/Arab Slave Trade followed by the <a href="/wiki/Mongol_invasions" title="Mongol invasions" class="mw-redirect">Mongol invasions</a> in the Middle East and Central Asia. <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South Asia</a> saw a series of <a href="/wiki/Middle_kingdoms_of_India" title="Middle kingdoms of India">middle kingdoms of India</a> followed by the establishment of <a href="/wiki/Islamic_empires_in_India" title="Islamic empires in India" class="mw-redirect">Islamic empires in India</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Mid-Imperial_China" title="Mid-Imperial China">Chinese Empire</a> saw the succession of the <a href="/wiki/Sui_Dynasty" title="Sui Dynasty">Sui</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tang_Dynasty" title="Tang Dynasty">Tang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liao_Dynasty" title="Liao Dynasty">Liao</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yuan_Dynasty" title="Yuan Dynasty">Yuan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ming_Dynasty" title="Ming Dynasty">Ming</a> Dynasties. During this period, Middle Eastern trade routes along the Indian Ocean and the <a href="/wiki/Silk_Road" title="Silk Road">Silk Road</a> through the Gobi Desert provided limited economic and cultural contact between Asian and European civilizations.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Black_Death" title="Black Death">Black Death</a> was one of the deadliest pandemics in human history. Starting in <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, the disease reached Mediterranean and western <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> during the late 1340s,<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76"><span>[</span>77<span>]</span></a></sup> and killed tens of millions of Europeans in six years; between a third and a half of the total population.<sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77"><span>[</span>78<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The Middle Ages<sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78"><span>[</span>79<span>]</span></a></sup> witnessed the first sustained <a href="/wiki/Urbanization" title="Urbanization">urbanization</a> of northern and western Europe. Many modern European states owe their origins to events unfolding in the Middle Ages; present European political boundaries are, in many regards, the result of the military and dynastic achievements during this tumultuous period.<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79"><span>[</span>80<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The Middle Ages lasted until the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Early_Modern_Period" title="Early Modern Period" class="mw-redirect">Early Modern Period</a><sup id="cite_ref-rice1970_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rice1970-17"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> in the 16th century, marked by the rise of <a href="/wiki/Nation-states" title="Nation-states" class="mw-redirect">nation-states</a>, the division of Western <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Protestant_Reformation" title="Protestant Reformation">Reformation</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80"><span>[</span>81<span>]</span></a></sup> the rise of <a href="/wiki/Humanism" title="Humanism">humanism</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Italian_Renaissance" title="Italian Renaissance">Italian Renaissance</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81"><span>[</span>82<span>]</span></a></sup> and the beginnings of European overseas expansion which allowed for the <a href="/wiki/Columbian_Exchange" title="Columbian Exchange">Columbian Exchange</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-82" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-82"><span>[</span>83<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_the_world&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Modern history">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_history">Modern history</span></h2>
<div class="rellink relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Modern_history" title="Modern history">Modern history</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Modern_history" title="Modern history">Modern history</a> (the "modern period," the "modern era," "modern times") is history of the period following the Middle Ages. "<a href="/wiki/Contemporary_history" title="Contemporary history">Contemporary history</a>" encompasses historic events that are immediately relevant to the present time; its intentionally loose <a href="/wiki/Ambit" title="Ambit">ambit</a> includes major events such as World War II, but not those whose immediate effects have dissipated.</p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_the_world&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Early Modern period">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_Modern_period">Early Modern period</span></h3>
<div class="rellink relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Early_Modern_period" title="Early Modern period" class="mw-redirect">Early Modern period</a></div>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/Early_modern_period" title="Early modern period">Early modern period</a>"<sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83"><span>[</span>84<span>]</span></a></sup> is a term used by historians to refer to the period in <a href="/wiki/Western_Europe" title="Western Europe">Western Europe</a> and its first <a href="/wiki/Colony" title="Colony">colonies</a> that spans the centuries between the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Revolution" title="Industrial Revolution">Industrial Revolution</a>. The early modern period is characterized by the rise to importance of <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">science</a> and by increasingly rapid <a href="/wiki/History_of_technology" title="History of technology">technological progress</a>, <a href="/wiki/Secular" title="Secular" class="mw-redirect">secularized</a> civic <a href="/wiki/Politics" title="Politics">politics</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Nation_state" title="Nation state">nation-state</a>. <a href="/wiki/Capitalist_economy" title="Capitalist economy" class="mw-redirect">Capitalist economies</a> began their rise, initially in northern <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republics</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Genoa" title="Genoa">Genoa</a>. The early modern period also saw the rise and dominance of the <a href="/wiki/Mercantilism" title="Mercantilism">mercantilist</a> economic theory. As such, the early modern period represents the decline and eventual disappearance, in much of the European sphere, of <a href="/wiki/Feudalism" title="Feudalism">feudalism</a>, serfdom and the power of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" title="Roman Catholic Church" class="mw-redirect">Catholic Church</a>. The period includes the <a href="/wiki/Protestant_Reformation" title="Protestant Reformation">Protestant Reformation</a>, the disastrous <a href="/wiki/Thirty_Years%27_War" title="Thirty Years' War">Thirty Years' War</a>, the <a href="/wiki/European_colonization_of_the_Americas" title="European colonization of the Americas">European colonization of the Americas</a>, and the peak of European <a href="/wiki/Witch-hunt" title="Witch-hunt">witch-hunting</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_the_world&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Rise of Europe">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Rise_of_Europe">Rise of Europe</span></h4>
<div class="rellink">Further information: <a href="/wiki/History_of_Europe" title="History of Europe">History of Europe</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Gutenburg_bible.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e3/Gutenburg_bible.jpg/220px-Gutenburg_bible.jpg" width="220" height="262" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Gutenburg_bible.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="http://bits.wikimedia.org/skins-1.5/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
The <a href="/wiki/Movable_type" title="Movable type">movable-type</a> <a href="/wiki/Printing_press" title="Printing press">printing press</a> arose in the mid-15th century. Less than 50 years later, nine million books were in print.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Nearly all the agricultural civilizations have been heavily constrained by their <a href="/wiki/Natural_environment" title="Natural environment">environments</a>. Productivity remained low, and <a href="/wiki/Climatic" title="Climatic" class="mw-redirect">climatic</a> changes easily instigated <a href="/wiki/Boom_and_bust" title="Boom and bust">boom and bust</a> <a href="/wiki/Business_cycle" title="Business cycle">cycles</a> that brought about civilizations' rise and fall. By about 1500, however, there was a qualitative change in world history. <a href="/wiki/Technological" title="Technological" class="mw-redirect">Technological</a> advance and the <a href="/wiki/Wealth" title="Wealth">wealth</a> generated by <a href="/wiki/Trade" title="Trade">trade</a> gradually brought about a widening of possibilities.<sup id="cite_ref-84" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-84"><span>[</span>85<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-85" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-85"><span>[</span>86<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-86" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-86"><span>[</span>87<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-87" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-87"><span>[</span>88<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-88" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-88"><span>[</span>89<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-89" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-89"><span>[</span>90<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-90" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-90"><span>[</span>91<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-91" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-91"><span>[</span>92<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-92" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-92"><span>[</span>93<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-93" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-93"><span>[</span>94<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-94" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-94"><span>[</span>95<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Outwardly, <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Renaissance" title="Renaissance">Renaissance</a>, beginning in the 14th century,<sup id="cite_ref-95" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-95"><span>[</span>96<span>]</span></a></sup> consisted in the rediscovery of the <a href="/wiki/Classical_antiquity" title="Classical antiquity">classical</a> world's scientific contributions, and in the <a href="/wiki/Economic" title="Economic" class="mw-redirect">economic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Social" title="Social">social</a> rise of Europe. But the Renaissance also engendered a culture of <a href="/wiki/Curiosity" title="Curiosity">inquisitiveness</a> which ultimately led to <a href="/wiki/Humanism" title="Humanism">Humanism</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-96" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-96"><span>[</span>97<span>]</span></a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Scientific_Revolution" title="Scientific Revolution" class="mw-redirect">Scientific Revolution</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-97" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-97"><span>[</span>98<span>]</span></a></sup> and finally the great transformation of the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Revolution" title="Industrial Revolution">Industrial Revolution</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Scientific_Revolution" title="Scientific Revolution" class="mw-redirect">Scientific Revolution</a> in the 17th century, however, had no immediate impact on <a href="/wiki/Technology" title="Technology">technology</a>; only in the second half of the 18th century did scientific advances begin to be applied to practical <a href="/wiki/Invention" title="Invention">invention</a>.</p>
<p>The advantages that Europe had developed by the mid-18th century were two: an <a href="/wiki/Entrepreneurial" title="Entrepreneurial" class="mw-redirect">entrepreneurial</a> culture,<sup class="Template-Fact" title="This claim needs references to reliable sources from August 2009" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">citation needed</a></i>]</sup> and the wealth generated by the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic" title="Atlantic" class="mw-redirect">Atlantic</a> trade (including the <a href="/wiki/African_slave_trade" title="African slave trade">African slave trade</a>). By the late 16th century, American <a href="/wiki/Silver" title="Silver">silver</a> accounted for one-fifth of Spain's total budget.<sup class="Template-Fact" title="This claim needs references to reliable sources from August 2009" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">citation needed</a></i>]</sup> The profits of the <a href="/wiki/Slave_trade" title="Slave trade" class="mw-redirect">slave trade</a> and of <a href="/wiki/West_Indies" title="West Indies" class="mw-redirect">West Indian</a> plantations amounted to 5% of the <a href="/wiki/Economic_history_of_Britain" title="Economic history of Britain" class="mw-redirect">British economy</a> at the time of the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Revolution" title="Industrial Revolution">Industrial Revolution</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-98" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-98"><span>[</span>99<span>]</span></a></sup> While some historians conclude that, in 1750, <a href="/wiki/Productivity" title="Productivity">labour productivity</a> in the most developed regions of China was still on a par with that of Europe's Atlantic economy (see the NBER Publications by Carol H. Shiue and Wolfgang Keller<sup id="cite_ref-99" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-99"><span>[</span>100<span>]</span></a></sup>), other historians like <a href="/wiki/Angus_Maddison" title="Angus Maddison">Angus Maddison</a> hold that the per-capita productivity of <a href="/wiki/Western_Europe" title="Western Europe">western Europe</a> had by the late <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a> surpassed that of all other regions.<sup id="cite_ref-100" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-100"><span>[</span>101<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>A number of explanations are proffered as to why, from the late Middle Ages on, Europe rose to surpass other civilizations, become the home of the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Revolution" title="Industrial Revolution">Industrial Revolution</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-101" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-101"><span>[</span>102<span>]</span></a></sup> and dominate the world. <a href="/wiki/Max_Weber" title="Max Weber">Max Weber</a> argued that it was due to a <a href="/wiki/Protestant_work_ethic" title="Protestant work ethic">Protestant work ethic</a> that encouraged Europeans to work harder and longer than others. Another socioeconomic explanation looks to <a href="/wiki/Medieval_demography" title="Medieval demography">demographics</a>: Europe, with its celibate clergy, colonial emigration, <a href="/wiki/Smallpox" title="Smallpox">high-mortality</a> urban centers, periodic <a href="/wiki/List_of_famines" title="List of famines">famines</a> and outbreaks of the <a href="/wiki/Black_Death" title="Black Death">Black Death</a>, continual <a href="/wiki/List_of_wars" title="List of wars" class="mw-redirect">warfare</a>, and late age of marriage had far more restrained <a href="/wiki/Population_growth" title="Population growth">population growth</a>, compared to Asian cultures. A relative shortage of labour meant that surpluses could be invested in labour-saving technological advances such as <a href="/wiki/Water-wheel" title="Water-wheel" class="mw-redirect">water-wheels</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mill_(factory)" title="Mill (factory)" class="mw-redirect">mills</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spinning_(textiles)" title="Spinning (textiles)">spinners</a> and <a href="/wiki/Loom" title="Loom">looms</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steam_engine" title="Steam engine">steam engines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shipping" title="Shipping">shipping</a>, rather than fueling population growth.</p>
<p>Many have also argued that Europe's institutions were superior,<sup id="cite_ref-102" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-102"><span>[</span>103<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-103" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-103"><span>[</span>104<span>]</span></a></sup> that <a href="/wiki/Property_right" title="Property right" class="mw-redirect">property rights</a> and <a href="/wiki/Free_market" title="Free market">free-market</a> economics were stronger than elsewhere due to an ideal of <a href="/wiki/Freedom_(political)" title="Freedom (political)" class="mw-redirect">freedom</a> peculiar to Europe. In recent years, however, scholars such as <a href="/wiki/Kenneth_Pomeranz" title="Kenneth Pomeranz">Kenneth Pomeranz</a> have challenged this view, although the revisionist approach to <a href="/wiki/World_history" title="World history" class="mw-redirect">world history</a> has also met with criticism for systematically "downplaying" European achievements.<sup id="cite_ref-104" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-104"><span>[</span>105<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Vascodagama.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="http://bits.wikimedia.org/skins-1.5/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Vasco_da_Gama" title="Vasco da Gama">Vasco da Gama</a> reached <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> by sea in 1498.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Europe's <a href="/wiki/Geography" title="Geography">geography</a> may also have played an important role. The <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> and <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> are all ringed by <a href="/wiki/Mountain" title="Mountain">mountains</a> but, once past these outer barriers, are relatively flat. By contrast, the <a href="/wiki/Pyrenees" title="Pyrenees">Pyrenees</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alps" title="Alps">Alps</a>, <a href="/wiki/Apennine_Mountains" title="Apennine Mountains">Apennines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carpathian_Mountains" title="Carpathian Mountains">Carpathians</a> and other mountain ranges run through Europe, and the continent is also divided by several <a href="/wiki/Sea" title="Sea">seas</a>. This gave Europe some degree of protection from the peril of <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asian</a> invaders. Before the era of firearms, these nomads were militarily superior to the agricultural states on the periphery of the <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">Eurasian</a> continent and, if they broke out into the plains of northern India or the valleys of China, were all but unstoppable. These invasions were often devastating. The <a href="/wiki/Islamic_Golden_Age" title="Islamic Golden Age">Golden Age of Islam</a><sup id="cite_ref-105" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-105"><span>[</span>106<span>]</span></a></sup> was ended by the <a href="/wiki/Mongol_invasions" title="Mongol invasions" class="mw-redirect">Mongol</a> sack of <a href="/wiki/Baghdad" title="Baghdad">Baghdad</a> in 1258. India and China were subject to periodic <a href="/wiki/Invasion" title="Invasion">invasions</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> spent a couple of centuries under the <a href="/wiki/Tatar_invasions" title="Tatar invasions" class="mw-redirect">Mongol-Tatar</a> Yoke. <a href="/wiki/Central_Europe" title="Central Europe">Central</a> and <a href="/wiki/Western_Europe" title="Western Europe">western Europe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Logistic" title="Logistic">logistically</a> more distant from the <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asian</a> heartland, proved less vulnerable to these threats.</p>
<p>Geography also contributed to important <a href="/wiki/Geopolitical" title="Geopolitical" class="mw-redirect">geopolitical</a> differences. For most of their histories, China, India and the Middle East were each unified under a single dominant power that expanded until it reached the surrounding mountains and deserts. In 1600 the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a><sup id="cite_ref-106" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-106"><span>[</span>107<span>]</span></a></sup> controlled almost all the Middle East, the <a href="/wiki/Ming_Dynasty" title="Ming Dynasty">Ming Dynasty</a> ruled China,<sup id="cite_ref-107" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-107"><span>[</span>108<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-108" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-108"><span>[</span>109<span>]</span></a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Mughal_Empire" title="Mughal Empire">Mughal Empire</a> held sway over India. By contrast, Europe was almost always divided into a number of warring states. Pan-European empires, with the notable exception of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a>, tended to collapse soon after they arose. Another doubtless important geographic factor in the rise of Europe was the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a>, which, for millennia, had functioned as a maritime superhighway fostering the exchange of goods, people, ideas and inventions.</p>
<h4><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_the_world&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Age of Discovery">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Age_of_Discovery">Age of Discovery</span></h4>
<div class="rellink relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Age_of_Discovery" title="Age of Discovery">Age of Discovery</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Eertvelt,_Santa_Maria.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/da/Eertvelt%2C_Santa_Maria.jpg/220px-Eertvelt%2C_Santa_Maria.jpg" width="220" height="263" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Eertvelt,_Santa_Maria.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="http://bits.wikimedia.org/skins-1.5/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Columbus</a> sought <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> aboard the <i><a href="/wiki/Santa_Maria_(ship)" title="Santa Maria (ship)" class="mw-redirect">Santa Maria</a></i> in 1492.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In the 14th century, the <a href="/wiki/Renaissance" title="Renaissance">Renaissance</a> began in Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-109" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-109"><span>[</span>110<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-110" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-110"><span>[</span>111<span>]</span></a></sup> Some modern scholars<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" title="The material in the vicinity of this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. from June 2009" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Avoid_weasel_words" title="Wikipedia:Avoid weasel words" class="mw-redirect">who?</a></i>]</sup> have questioned whether this flowering of <a href="/wiki/Art" title="Art">art</a> and <a href="/wiki/Humanism" title="Humanism">Humanism</a> was a benefit to science. The era did see an important fusion of Arab and European knowledge.<sup id="cite_ref-111" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-111"><span>[</span>112<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-112" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-112"><span>[</span>113<span>]</span></a></sup> One of the most important developments was the <a href="/wiki/Caravel" title="Caravel">caravel</a>, which combined the Mediterranean <a href="/wiki/Lateen_sail" title="Lateen sail" class="mw-redirect">lateen sail</a> with European <a href="/wiki/Square_rig" title="Square rig">square rigging</a> to create the first vessels that could safely sail the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-113" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-113"><span>[</span>114<span>]</span></a></sup> Along with important developments in <a href="/wiki/Navigation" title="Navigation">navigation</a>, this technology allowed the <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a> in 1492 to journey across the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> and bridge the gap between <a href="/wiki/Afro-Eurasia" title="Afro-Eurasia">Afro-Eurasia</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Americas" title="The Americas" class="mw-redirect">the Americas</a>.</p>
<p>This had dramatic effects on both continents. The Europeans brought with them <a href="/wiki/Viral" title="Viral">viral</a> diseases that American natives had never encountered,<sup id="cite_ref-114" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-114"><span>[</span>115<span>]</span></a></sup> and uncertain numbers of natives died in a series of devastating <a href="/wiki/Epidemic" title="Epidemic">epidemics</a>. The Europeans also had the technological advantage of <a href="/wiki/Horse" title="Horse">horses</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steel" title="Steel">steel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gun" title="Gun">guns</a> that helped them overpower the <a href="/wiki/Aztec" title="Aztec">Aztec</a> and <a href="/wiki/Incan" title="Incan" class="mw-redirect">Incan</a> empires as well as <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North American</a> cultures.<sup id="cite_ref-115" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-115"><span>[</span>116<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Gold and resources from the Americas began to be stripped from the land and people and shipped to Europe, while at the same time large numbers of European colonists began to emigrate to the Americas.<sup id="cite_ref-116" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-116"><span>[</span>117<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-117" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-117"><span>[</span>118<span>]</span></a></sup> To meet the great demand for labor in the new colonies, the mass import of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africans</a> as <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slaves</a> began.<sup id="cite_ref-118" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-118"><span>[</span>119<span>]</span></a></sup> Soon much of the Americas had a large racial underclass of slaves. In <a href="/wiki/West_Africa" title="West Africa">West Africa</a>, a series of thriving states developed along the <a href="/wiki/Slave_Coast" title="Slave Coast">coast</a>, becoming prosperous from the exploitation of suffering interior African peoples.</p>
<p>Europe's maritime expansion unsurprisingly&#160;— given that continent's geography&#160;— was largely the work of its Atlantic states: <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Empire" title="Portuguese Empire">Portuguese</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish Empires</a> were the predominant conquerors and source of influence, and their union resulted in the <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Union" title="Iberian Union">Iberian Union</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-119" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-119"><span>[</span>120<span>]</span></a></sup> the first <a href="/wiki/Global_empire" title="Global empire" class="mw-redirect">global empire</a>, on which the "<a href="/wiki/The_empire_on_which_the_sun_never_sets" title="The empire on which the sun never sets">sun never set</a>". Soon the more northern <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> and <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a> began to dominate the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_(ocean)" title="Atlantic (ocean)" class="mw-redirect">Atlantic</a>. In a series of wars fought in the 17th and 18th centuries, culminating with the <a href="/wiki/Napoleonic_Wars" title="Napoleonic Wars">Napoleonic Wars</a>, Britain emerged as the new world power.</p>
<p>Meanwhile the voyages of Admiral <a href="/wiki/Zheng_He" title="Zheng He">Zheng He</a> were halted by China's <a href="/wiki/Ming_Dynasty" title="Ming Dynasty">Ming Dynasty</a> (1368–1644), established after the expulsion of the <a href="/wiki/Mongols" title="Mongols">Mongols</a>. A Chinese commercial revolution, sometimes described as "incipient <a href="/wiki/Capitalism" title="Capitalism">capitalism</a>", was also abortive. The <a href="/wiki/Ming_Dynasty" title="Ming Dynasty">Ming Dynasty</a> would eventually fall to the <a href="/wiki/Manchu" title="Manchu">Manchus</a>, whose <a href="/wiki/Qing_Dynasty" title="Qing Dynasty">Qing Dynasty</a> at first oversaw a period of calm and prosperity but would increasingly fall prey to Western encroachment.</p>

<div class="rellink relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/19th_century" title="19th century">19th century</a></div>
<p>After Europeans had achieved dominance over the Americas, their <a href="/wiki/Imperialism" title="Imperialism">imperial</a> appetites turned to the countries of Asia. In the 19th century the European states had a distinct technological advantage over Asian states and peoples. Britain gained control of the <a href="/wiki/Indian_subcontinent" title="Indian subcontinent">Indian subcontinent</a>, <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Peninsular_Malaysia" title="Peninsular Malaysia">Malay Peninsula</a>; the French took <a href="/wiki/French_Indochina" title="French Indochina">Indochina</a>; while the Dutch cemented their control over the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_Indies" title="Dutch East Indies">Dutch East Indies</a>. In addition, Russia colonised large pre-agricultural areas of Siberia. The British also colonised places inhabited by <a href="/wiki/Neolithic" title="Neolithic">Neolithic</a> peoples, including <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>. large numbers of British colonists emigrated to these colonies. In the late 19th century, the European powers <a href="/wiki/Scramble_for_Africa" title="Scramble for Africa">divided</a> the remaining areas of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. Within Europe, economic and military challenges created a system of <a href="/wiki/Nation_state" title="Nation state">nation states</a>, and ethno-linguistic groupings began to identify themselves as distinctive <a href="/wiki/Nation" title="Nation">nations</a> with aspirations for cultural and political autonomy. This <a href="/wiki/Nationalism" title="Nationalism">nationalism</a> would become important to peoples across the world in the 20th century.</p>
<p>This era in European culture saw the <a href="/wiki/Age_of_Reason" title="Age of Reason" class="mw-redirect">Age of Reason</a> lead to the <a href="/wiki/Scientific_Revolution" title="Scientific Revolution" class="mw-redirect">Scientific Revolution</a>. The Scientific Revolution changed humanity's understanding of the world and happened simultaneously with the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Revolution" title="Industrial Revolution">Industrial Revolution</a>, a major transformation of the world's economies. The Industrial Revolution began in <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland" title="United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland">Great Britain</a> and used new modes of production&#160;— the <a href="/wiki/Factory" title="Factory">factory</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mass_production" title="Mass production">mass production</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mechanisation" title="Mechanisation" class="mw-redirect">mechanisation</a>&#160;— to manufacture a wide array of goods faster and using less labour than previously.</p>
<p>The Age of Reason also led to the beginnings of modern <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democracy</a> in the late-18th century <a href="/wiki/American_Revolution" title="American Revolution">American</a> and <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French Revolutions</a>. Democracy would grow to have a profound effect on world events and on <a href="/wiki/Quality_of_life" title="Quality of life">quality of life</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" title="The material in the vicinity of this tag may not be factual or accurate from August 2009" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Disputed_statement" title="Wikipedia:Disputed statement">dubious</a> <span class="metadata">– <a href="/wiki/Talk:History_of_the_world#Dubious" title="Talk:History of the world">discuss</a></span></i>]</sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" title="This statement may not be entirely without bias from August 2009" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Neutral_point_of_view" title="Wikipedia:Neutral point of view">neutrality</a> is <a href="/wiki/Talk:History_of_the_world" title="Talk:History of the world">disputed</a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>During the Industrial Revolution, the world economy became reliant on <a href="/wiki/Coal" title="Coal">coal</a> as a fuel, as new methods of <a href="/wiki/Transport" title="Transport">transport</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Railways" title="Railways" class="mw-redirect">railways</a> and <a href="/wiki/Steamship" title="Steamship" class="mw-redirect">steamships</a>, effectively shrank the world. Meanwhile, industrial <a href="/wiki/Pollution" title="Pollution">pollution</a> and <a href="/wiki/Natural_environment" title="Natural environment">environmental</a> damage, present since the discovery of fire and the beginning of civilization, accelerated drastically.</p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_the_world&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: 20th century to present">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="20th_century_to_present">20th century to present</span></h3>
<div class="rellink relarticle mainarticle">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/20th_century" title="20th century">20th century</a> and <a href="/wiki/21st_century" title="21st century">21st century</a></div>
<h4><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_the_world&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Early 20th century">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_20th_century">Early 20th century</span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:WWI.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/WWI.png/220px-WWI.png" width="220" height="102" class="thumbimage" /></a>

<a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>, fought by the <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_I" title="Allies of World War I">Allies</a> <i>(green)</i> and <a href="/wiki/Central_Powers" title="Central Powers">Central Powers</a> <i>(orange)</i>, ended the <a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">German</a>, <a href="/wiki/Austro-Hungary" title="Austro-Hungary" class="mw-redirect">Austro-Hungarian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empires</a>.</div>

<p>The 20th century<sup id="cite_ref-120" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-120"><span>[</span>121<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-121" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-121"><span>[</span>122<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-122" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-122"><span>[</span>123<span>]</span></a></sup> opened with <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> at an apex of wealth and power, and with much of the world under its direct <a href="/wiki/Colonialism" title="Colonialism">colonial</a> control or its indirect domination.<sup id="cite_ref-123" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-123"><span>[</span>124<span>]</span></a></sup> Much of the rest of the world was influenced by heavily Europeanized nations: the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-124" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-124"><span>[</span>125<span>]</span></a></sup> As the century unfolded, however, the global system dominated by rival powers was subjected to severe strains, and ultimately yielded to a more fluid structure of independent nations organized on Western models.</p>
<p>This transformation was <a href="/wiki/Catalysis" title="Catalysis">catalysed</a> by <a href="/wiki/War" title="War">wars</a> of unparalleled scope and devastation. <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a><sup id="cite_ref-125" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-125"><span>[</span>126<span>]</span></a></sup> destroyed many of Europe's empires and monarchies, and weakened <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland" title="United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland">Britain</a> and <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-126" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-126"><span>[</span>127<span>]</span></a></sup> In its aftermath, powerful ideologies arose. The <a href="/wiki/Russian_Revolution_(1917)" title="Russian Revolution (1917)">Russian Revolution</a><sup id="cite_ref-127" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-127"><span>[</span>128<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-128" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-128"><span>[</span>129<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-129" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-129"><span>[</span>130<span>]</span></a></sup> of 1917 created the first <a href="/wiki/Communism" title="Communism">communist</a> state, while the 1920s and 1930s saw <a href="/wiki/Militarism" title="Militarism">militaristic</a> <a href="/wiki/Fascism" title="Fascism">fascist</a> dictatorships gain control in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> and elsewhere.<sup id="cite_ref-130" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-130"><span>[</span>131<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Ongoing national rivalries, exacerbated by the economic turmoil of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Depression" title="Great Depression">Great Depression</a>, helped precipitate <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-131" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-131"><span>[</span>132<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-132" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-132"><span>[</span>133<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Militaristic" title="Militaristic" class="mw-redirect">militaristic</a> <a href="/wiki/Dictatorship" title="Dictatorship">dictatorships</a> of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> pursued an ultimately doomed course of <a href="/wiki/Imperialist" title="Imperialist" class="mw-redirect">imperialist</a> <a href="/wiki/Expansionism" title="Expansionism">expansionism</a>. Their defeat opened the way for the advance of <a href="/wiki/Communism" title="Communism">communism</a> into <a href="/wiki/Central_Europe" title="Central Europe">Central Europe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yugoslavia" title="Yugoslavia">Yugoslavia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albania</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Vietnam" title="North Vietnam">North Vietnam</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a>.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nagasakibomb.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Nagasakibomb.jpg/220px-Nagasakibomb.jpg" width="220" height="263" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nagasakibomb.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="http://bits.wikimedia.org/skins-1.5/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Nuclear_weapon" title="Nuclear weapon">Nuclear bombs</a>, dropped on <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> in 1945, ended <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> and opened the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Following World War II, in 1945, the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> was founded in the hope of allaying conflicts among nations and preventing future wars.<sup id="cite_ref-133" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-133"><span>[</span>134<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-134" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-134"><span>[</span>135<span>]</span></a></sup> The war had, however, left two nations, the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a><sup id="cite_ref-135" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-135"><span>[</span>136<span>]</span></a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>, with principal power to guide international affairs.<sup id="cite_ref-136" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-136"><span>[</span>137<span>]</span></a></sup> Each was suspicious of the other and feared a global spread of the other's political-economic model. This led to the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a>, a forty-year stand-off between the United States, the Soviet Union, and their respective allies. With the development of <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_weapons" title="Nuclear weapons" class="mw-redirect">nuclear weapons</a><sup id="cite_ref-137" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-137"><span>[</span>138<span>]</span></a></sup> and the subsequent <a href="/wiki/Arms_race" title="Arms race">arms race</a>, all of humanity were put at risk of <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_warfare" title="Nuclear warfare">nuclear war</a> between the two superpowers.<sup id="cite_ref-138" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-138"><span>[</span>139<span>]</span></a></sup> Such war being viewed as impractical, <a href="/wiki/Proxy_war" title="Proxy war">proxy wars</a> were instead waged, at the expense of non-nuclear-armed <a href="/wiki/Third_World" title="Third World">Third World</a> countries.</p>
<h4><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_the_world&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Late 20th century">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Late_20th_century">Late 20th century</span></h4>
<p>The Cold War lasted to the 1990s, when the Soviet Union's communist system began to collapse, unable to compete economically with the United States and western Europe; the Soviets' <a href="/wiki/Central_Europe" title="Central Europe">Central European</a> "<a href="/wiki/Satellite_state" title="Satellite state">satellites</a>" reasserted their national sovereignty, and in 1991 the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> itself <a href="/wiki/Breakup_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Breakup of the Soviet Union" class="mw-redirect">disintegrated</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-139" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-139"><span>[</span>140<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-140" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-140"><span>[</span>141<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-141" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-141"><span>[</span>142<span>]</span></a></sup> The United States for the time being was left as the "sole remaining superpower".<sup id="cite_ref-142" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-142"><span>[</span>143<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-143" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-143"><span>[</span>144<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-144" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-144"><span>[</span>145<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In the early postwar decades, the <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">African</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asian</a> colonies of the Belgian, British, Dutch, French and other west European empires won their formal independence.<sup id="cite_ref-145" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-145"><span>[</span>146<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-146" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-146"><span>[</span>147<span>]</span></a></sup> These nations faced challenges in the form of <a href="/wiki/Neocolonialism" title="Neocolonialism">neocolonialism</a>, poverty, illiteracy and <a href="/wiki/Endemic_(epidemiology)" title="Endemic (epidemiology)">endemic</a> <a href="/wiki/Tropical_disease" title="Tropical disease">tropical diseases</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-147" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-147"><span>[</span>148<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-148" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-148"><span>[</span>149<span>]</span></a></sup> Many of the <a href="/wiki/Western_Europe" title="Western Europe">Western</a> and <a href="/wiki/Central_Europe" title="Central Europe">Central European</a> nations gradually formed a political and economic community, the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>, which subsequently expanded eastward to include former Soviet satellites.<sup id="cite_ref-149" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-149"><span>[</span>150<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-150" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-150"><span>[</span>151<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-151" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-151"><span>[</span>152<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-152" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-152"><span>[</span>153<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Apollo_17_Cernan_on_moon.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Apollo_17_Cernan_on_moon.jpg/220px-Apollo_17_Cernan_on_moon.jpg" width="220" height="221" class="thumbimage" /></a>

<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Apollo_17_Cernan_on_moon.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="http://bits.wikimedia.org/skins-1.5/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
Last <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">Moon</a> landing — <a href="/wiki/Apollo_17" title="Apollo 17">Apollo 17</a> (1972)</div>

<p>The 20th century saw exponential progress in <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">science</a> and <a href="/wiki/Technology" title="Technology">technology</a>, and increased <a href="/wiki/Life_expectancy" title="Life expectancy">life expectancy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Standard_of_living" title="Standard of living">standard of living</a> for much of humanity. As the developed world shifted from a <a href="/wiki/Coal" title="Coal">coal</a>-based to a <a href="/wiki/Petroleum" title="Petroleum">petroleum</a>-based economy, new transport technologies, along with the dawn of the <a href="/wiki/Information_Age" title="Information Age">Information Age</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-153" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-153"><span>[</span>154<span>]</span></a></sup> led to increased <a href="/wiki/Globalization" title="Globalization">globalization</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-154" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-154"><span>[</span>155<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-155" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-155"><span>[</span>156<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-156" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-156"><span>[</span>157<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Space_exploration" title="Space exploration">Space exploration</a> reached throughout the <a href="/wiki/Solar_system" title="Solar system" class="mw-redirect">solar system</a>. The structure of <a href="/wiki/DNA" title="DNA">DNA</a>, the very template of <a href="/wiki/Life" title="Life">life</a>, was discovered,<sup id="cite_ref-157" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-157"><span>[</span>158<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-158" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-158"><span>[</span>159<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-159" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-159"><span>[</span>160<span>]</span></a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Human_genome" title="Human genome">human genome</a> was sequenced, a major milestone in the understanding of <a href="/wiki/Human_biology" title="Human biology">human biology</a> and the treatment of <a href="/wiki/Disease" title="Disease">disease</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-160" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-160"><span>[</span>161<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-161" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-161"><span>[</span>162<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-162" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-162"><span>[</span>163<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-163" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-163"><span>[</span>164<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-164" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-164"><span>[</span>165<span>]</span></a></sup> Global <a href="/wiki/Literacy" title="Literacy">literacy</a> rates continued to rise, and the percentage of the world's <a href="/wiki/Labour_(economics)" title="Labour (economics)" class="mw-redirect">labor</a> pool needed to produce <a href="/wiki/Humankind" title="Humankind" class="mw-redirect">humankind</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">food</a> supply continued to drop.</p>
<p>The technologies of sound recordings, motion pictures, and radio and television broadcasting produced a focus on popular culture and entertainment. Television spots sold both commercial products and political candidates. Some worried that the young generation was losing basic literacy skills. Then, in the last decade of this century, a rapid increase took place in the use of personal computers. A global communication network emerged in the Internet. Mass entertainment gave way to individual communication in what has been called a shift from the fourth to a fifth civilization.<sup id="cite_ref-165" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-165"><span>[</span>166<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The century saw the development of new global threats, such as <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_proliferation" title="Nuclear proliferation">nuclear proliferation</a>, worldwide <a href="/wiki/Epidemic" title="Epidemic">epidemics</a> of diseases, <a href="/wiki/Global_climate_change" title="Global climate change" class="mw-redirect">global climate change</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-166" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-166"><span>[</span>167<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-167" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-167"><span>[</span>168<span>]</span></a></sup> massive <a href="/wiki/Deforestation" title="Deforestation">deforestation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Overpopulation" title="Overpopulation">overpopulation</a>, and the dwindling of global <a href="/wiki/Natural_resource" title="Natural resource">resources</a> (particularly <a href="/wiki/Fossil_fuel" title="Fossil fuel">fossil fuels</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-168" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-168"><span>[</span>169<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_the_world&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: 21st century">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="21st_century">21st century</span></h4>
<div class="rellink relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_history" title="Contemporary history">Contemporary history</a></div>
<p>The 21st century has been marked by rising <a href="/wiki/Terrorism" title="Terrorism">terrorism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stress_(biology)" title="Stress (biology)">stress</a>, the expansion of <a href="/wiki/Economic_globalization" title="Economic globalization">economic globalization</a>, the expansion of communications and telecommunications with <a href="/wiki/Mobile_phone" title="Mobile phone">mobile phones</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a>, and international pop culture. Worldwide <a href="/wiki/Demand" title="Demand" class="mw-redirect">demand</a> and <a href="/wiki/Competition" title="Competition">competition</a> for <a href="/wiki/Natural_resource" title="Natural resource">resources</a> rose due to growing populations and industrialization, mainly in India, China and <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. This demand is resulting in increased levels of environmental degradation and a growing threat of <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">global warming</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-169" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-169"><span>[</span>170<span>]</span></a></sup> This in turn has spurred the development of alternate or <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">renewable</a> sources of <a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">energy</a>, proposals for cleaner <a href="/wiki/Fossil_fuels" title="Fossil fuels" class="mw-redirect">fossil-fuel</a> technologies, and consideration of expanded use of <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_energy" title="Nuclear energy" class="mw-redirect">nuclear energy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-170" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-170"><span>[</span>171<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-171" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-171"><span>[</span>172<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-172" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-172"><span>[</span>173<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
